# Scaling Limits and the Research Frontier

## Definition
The emerging consensus that scaling up model size, data, and compute (the dominant AI strategy from ~2020–2025) is hitting diminishing returns, and that the next phase of AI progress will require fundamentally new research paradigms — synthetic data, continual learning, new architectures, and solving the generalization problem.

## Key Points
- Sutskever: "The data is finite. There is only one internet. Pre-training as we have known it is over." Three eras: 2012–2020 research-driven, 2020–2025 scaling-driven, 2026+ research-driven again [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-ilya-sutskever-2]])
- Patel: RL on top of LLMs won't achieve AGI — "mid-training" in fabricated environments is a sign we lack the core of genuine learning; draws explicit expert systems parallel [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- Generalization gap: current models "generalize dramatically worse than people" — they excel at benchmarks through pattern matching, not flexible learning [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-ilya-sutskever-2]])
- Economic impact of AI is slower than claimed because generalization and learning autonomy gaps prevent widespread automation [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- Next-generation approaches: synthetic data, self-generated training problems, continual learning, more generalizable architectures [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-ilya-sutskever-2]])
- "Straight shot" scaling of transformers hitting diminishing returns; breakthroughs require fundamentally new algorithms [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-ilya-sutskever-2]])
- Patel is "moderately bearish short-term, explosively bullish long-term" — the inflection requires paradigm shifts, not incremental scaling [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])

## Open Questions
- If pre-training as we know it is over, how does this change the compute demand curve? Does it reduce demand (less brute-force training) or redirect it (more autoresearch loops, synthetic data generation)?
- Does SSI's research-first, no-product strategy represent the future model for AI labs, or is it a luxury only available with Sutskever-level reputation and funding?
- If the generalization gap is the binding constraint, is the current investment in bigger models and more GPUs misallocated relative to research investment?
- What are the implications for GitHub/Microsoft if the next AI breakthroughs are algorithmic rather than compute-driven — does this shift competitive advantage away from hyperscalers?
