# AGI Timelines and Progress Debates

## Definition
The ongoing dispute about when (and whether) current AI scaling trajectories lead to Artificial General Intelligence — covering arguments about the sufficiency of current RL/LLM approaches, the nature of general-purpose learning, and geopolitical implications of fast vs. slow AI timelines.

## Key Points
- "Fast timelines favor the US; slow timelines favor China" — the geopolitical stakes of AI timeline uncertainty are asymmetric; US leads on frontier model capability but China benefits if progress stalls and hardware indigenization catches up ([[dwarkesh-dylan-patel-interview]])
- Current RL-on-LLM approaches may be a "large-scale reprise of the expert systems era": instead of hand-coded rules, labs now hand-craft RL training environments — and the resulting models still lack flexible, on-the-job learning [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- Robotics as a falsification test: genuine human-level generalization would not require thousands of in-home demonstrations; the data hunger of current robot learning reveals a fundamental learning gap [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- "Economic diffusion lag" explanations for limited AI macro impact may be "cope" — the honest explanation is that current systems lack the learning flexibility needed to truly substitute for high-context, trust-dependent human labor [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- Goalpost-shifting on AGI definitions is epistemically justified as evidence about scaling limits accumulates — not motivated skepticism [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])
- Long-run "intelligence explosion" is still credible: once flexible general learning is achieved, recursive self-improvement could be very rapid [UNVERIFIED] ([[dwarkesh-thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025]])

## Open Questions
- Are current RL-on-LLM approaches a dead end, or a stepping stone to more general learning algorithms? No source models what a "post-RL-LLM" architecture would look like.
- If Patel's skepticism is correct, what are the implications for the massive CapEx commitments documented in [[dwarkesh-dylan-patel-interview]] and [[great-gpu-shortage-rental-capacity]]? Are hyperscalers building infrastructure for a paradigm that will need to be replaced?
- Does the "slow timelines" scenario change the strategic calculus for Microsoft/GitHub's AI product roadmap? If AGI is farther away, Copilot and agent products compete on workflow value rather than racing toward autonomy.
- How do we distinguish "pre-AGI agents doing valuable narrow work" from "AGI"? The answer determines whether GitHub's developer workflow position is defensible long-term or will be disrupted by a capability step-change.
