# Clouded Judgement 12.5.25 - Monopoly Money

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-12525-monopoly
**Date**: December 5, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball warns that startup employees are increasingly valuing equity packages by present dollar value rather than percentage ownership, driving founders to raise at inflated valuations just to remain competitive on talent. This repeats the 2021 cycle where paper marks disconnected from business fundamentals, eventually leaving employees with underwater options. The dynamic creates a structural incentive for valuation inflation that increases risk for both founders and employees while benefiting investors playing power-law portfolios.

## Key Claims

- Employees consistently prefer a $10M equity package at a $3B valuation over a $5M package at a $500M valuation, ignoring that the latter offers more upside.
- Options issued at higher strike prices have a higher probability of being worthless at exit.
- This behavior mirrors the 2021 startup equity bubble, where inflated marks led to mass employee attrition when equity went underwater.
- Investors can absorb this risk via power-law portfolio dynamics; founders with a single company cannot.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue multiple stood at 4.8x as of early December 2025, with high-growth median at 15.1x.
- Median SaaS net retention was 108%, with median FCF margin at 19%.
