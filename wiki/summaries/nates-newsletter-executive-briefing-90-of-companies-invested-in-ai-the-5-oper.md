# Executive Briefing: 90% of Companies Invested in AI. The 5 Operations Separating the 40% Who Got Results from Everyone Else.

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/executive-briefing-90-of-companies
**Date**: March 1, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Introduces the concept of "frontier operations" -- a new workforce meta-skill that decomposes into five simultaneous practices: boundary sensing, seam design, failure model maintenance, capability forecasting, and leverage calibration. Argues that unlike prior workforce skills (literacy, numeracy, coding) which were destinations, this skill has no fixed endpoint because the AI capability surface keeps expanding. The 90% adoption / <40% impact gap exists not because companies chose wrong tools but because the skill required to convert capability into output has no name, no curriculum, and no precedent in workforce development.

## Key Claims

- 90% of companies report investing in AI; fewer than 40% report meaningful bottom-line impact.
- "Frontier operations" decomposes into five simultaneous operations: boundary sensing, seam design, failure model maintenance, capability forecasting, and leverage calibration.
- Every prior workforce skill (literacy, numeracy, coding) was a destination; frontier operations has no fixed endpoint because AI capabilities keep expanding.
- As the AI capability "bubble" inflates, its surface area increases -- creating more boundary to operate at, not less.
- A person who started building this skill six months ago is not just six months ahead but operating in a different technological era.
- Japan, South Korea, and Germany need frontier operations capacity most and are least equipped to build it; Singapore is the outlier.
- Two organizational units are emerging in frontier-capable companies: "Team of One" and "Team of Five."
- Traditional hiring signals are nearly useless for identifying frontier operators.
