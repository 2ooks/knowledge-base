# 200 Lines of Markdown Just Triggered a $285 Billion Sell-Off

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/200-lines-of-markdown-just-triggered
**Date**: February 10, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Anthropic's open-source Claude Cowork legal plugin -- roughly 200 lines of structured markdown -- catalyzed a $285B single-session collapse across enterprise software, legal information, and financial services stocks. The article argues the plugin did not cause the crash but revealed a structural crack: per-seat SaaS pricing is broken when AI agents can do work without logging in. The survival path requires pivoting from UI-first to agentic-first architecture and repricing around data and accountability rather than headcount. KPMG already used AI as fee negotiation leverage to cut audit fees 14%, establishing a playbook that will cascade across professional services.

## Key Claims

- Thomson Reuters fell ~18%, LexisNexis parent RELX ~14%, Wolters Kluwer ~13%, LegalZoom ~20% in a single session
- Software sector forward P/E compressed from ~39x to ~21x -- steepest since 2002 dot-com bust
- KPMG pressured Grant Thornton to cut audit fees 14% (from $416K to $357K) using AI as negotiation leverage
- Jensen Huang's counterargument (AI needs more software) defends the product but misses the pricing model attack
- Per-seat SaaS model breaks when AI agents replace 10 paralegals with 10 Westlaw logins -- data value persists, 9 seats of revenue vanish
- The "articulation problem" (translating vague human intent into workable software) is the real bottleneck for build-vs-buy disruption
- Cursor shipped 1,000 code commits/hour with no human involvement; StrongDM mandated no human code writing or reviewing
- Opus 4.6 5x'd working memory in a single week; Claude Cowork went from demo to $285B market event in 48 hours
