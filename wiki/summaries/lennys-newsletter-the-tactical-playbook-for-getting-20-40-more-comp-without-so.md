# The Tactical Playbook for Getting 20-40% More Comp

**Source URL**: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-tactical-playbook-for-getting-more-comp
**Date**: March 15, 2026
**Publication**: Lenny's Newsletter (Podcast)

## Summary

Executive negotiation coach Jacob Warwick shares a framework for senior tech operators to negotiate 20-40% higher compensation packages. His core thesis is that negotiation starts before the offer -- during the interview process itself -- and that product people systematically negotiate worse than peers in other functions. While not directly AI-related, the piece is relevant to BD in that it maps negotiation dynamics (information asymmetry, timing leverage, BATNA/ZOPA frameworks) that are directly applicable to partnership structuring. The mention of AI tools reshaping job markets adds context for how compensation dynamics are shifting.

## Key Claims

- A simple phrase ("What's the chance there's a little more here?") often unlocks a 20% compensation bump
- Well-run negotiations see 40% average movement from initial offer
- Negotiation begins during the interview process, not at the offer stage
- Information and timing are the two primary sources of negotiating power
- Product managers negotiate worse than peers in other functions
- Best interviews feel like discovery calls rather than interrogations
- The "What's your comp expectation?" question is the most critical to navigate without anchoring low
- Collaboration-based negotiation consistently outperforms confrontational approaches
