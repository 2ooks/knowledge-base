# Clouded Judgement 5.30.25 - Moats in the Age of AI

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-53025-moats-in
**Date**: May 30, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball argues that durable moats are largely a myth in AI -- what looks like a long-term moat is really a sequence of time-bound advantages stacked together. The moat window has compressed from 6-12 months to 2-3 weeks as models shift, infrastructure evolves, and competitors can replicate product head starts with "3 prompts and a wrapper." Speed of execution -- building, shipping, learning, killing bad ideas, and adapting faster than everyone else -- is the only sustainable edge. Categories like LLM CRMs, AI note-taking tools, and vector databases are already seeing differentiation collapse overnight.

## Key Claims

- The moat window has compressed from 6-12 months to 2-3 weeks in the AI era.
- A 12-month product head start in 2022 is now "3 prompts and a wrapper" away from replication.
- LLM-based CRMs, AI note-taking tools, and vector databases have all experienced rapid differentiation collapse.
- Speed is the moat: execution speed, hiring speed, firing speed, distribution speed, decision-making speed.
- The "long-term moat" is actually a sequence of smaller, time-bound advantages stacked in series.
- Killing bad ideas quickly is as important as executing good ones fast -- opportunity cost of time has never been greater.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue at 5.6x (May 2025); high-growth median at 20.6x.
- Median CAC payback at 45 months, suggesting slowing efficiency in customer acquisition.
