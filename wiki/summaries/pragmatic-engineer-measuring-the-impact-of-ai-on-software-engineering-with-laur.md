# Measuring the Impact of AI on Software Engineering with Laura Tacho

**Source URL**: https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/measuring-the-impact-of-ai-on-software
**Date**: July 23, 2025
**Publication**: The Pragmatic Engineer

## Summary

Laura Tacho, CTO of DX, presents findings from 180+ companies on AI's actual impact on developer productivity, arguing that most measurement frameworks (acceptance rate, lines of code) are inadequate and that the real time savings are far smaller than headlines suggest. The key insight for BD: average engineers spend only ~20-25% of their time coding, so even a 10% improvement in coding speed yields marginal total productivity gains. Companies winning with AI are those shifting from roadmap-sequential thinking to experiment-portfolio models. This directly informs how to frame AI developer tool value propositions to enterprise buyers.

## Key Claims

- DX research spans 180+ companies measuring AI's impact on developer productivity
- Average engineers spend only 20-25% of their time coding; AWS found their engineers spend only 20%
- AI time savings applied to 20% of work time don't translate to dramatic overall productivity gains
- Code acceptance rate and lines of code are insufficient measures of AI productivity impact
- "Source code is a liability" -- more code generated faster doesn't equal better outcomes
- Roadmaps are "on their way out" in favor of experiment portfolios for companies winning with AI
- Highly regulated industries are seeing the best results with structured AI rollouts
- Booking.com case study used as example of measured AI tool rollout
- AI-generated documentation differs meaningfully from human-written docs
- DORA report on AI in engineering showed "surprising results" about actual versus expected impact
