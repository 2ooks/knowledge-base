# Thoughts on AI Progress (Dec 2025) — Dwarkesh Patel
- **Source:** https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025-video
- **Date:** December 2025
- **Author:** Dwarkesh Patel

## Summary
Dwarkesh Patel's December 2025 essay argues he is "moderately bearish" on near-term AI progress while remaining "explosively bullish" in the long run. [UNVERIFIED] His central claim is that current RL-on-LLM approaches — where labs build expensive, curated RL environments to teach models specific tools (Excel, browsers, coding workflows) — are a reprise of the expert systems era: instead of hand-coding rules, experts now hand-craft training examples, and the resulting models still lack the flexible, general-purpose learning that a true AGI would exhibit. [UNVERIFIED] He uses robotics as a stress test: if AI were genuinely human-level in generalization, robots would not need to be trained across thousands of homes to master tasks like dishwashing; the fact that they do reveals a fundamental gap in transfer learning. [UNVERIFIED] Patel also challenges "economic diffusion lag" as an explanation for AI's limited macro impact, arguing the more honest answer is that current systems lack critical learning capabilities, not just deployment reach. [UNVERIFIED] He is skeptical of bootstrapping arguments — the idea that a superhuman AI researcher, once built via RL, would rapidly solve AGI — because current models do not yet demonstrate the generalization needed to reliably self-improve. [UNVERIFIED]

## Key Claims
- Current RL-on-LLM scaling is "baking in" task-specific skills the same way expert systems hand-coded domain rules — a sign we have not achieved general-purpose learning [UNVERIFIED]
- A true AGI would not require extensive tool-specific RL environments; humans learn new tools on the job without curated training curricula [UNVERIFIED]
- Robotics: AI systems still need thousands of in-home demonstrations for basic manipulation — fundamentally inconsistent with "human-level" generalization [UNVERIFIED]
- "Economic diffusion lag is cope" — the real bottleneck is a technical gap in learning capability, not slow enterprise adoption [UNVERIFIED]
- "Goal-post shifting is justified" — as we learn more about scaling limits, revising timeline expectations downward is epistemically honest rather than motivated skepticism [UNVERIFIED]
- Long-term bullishness is intact: once the field achieves robust, flexible learning, the subsequent intelligence explosion could be very rapid [UNVERIFIED]
