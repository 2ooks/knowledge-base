## Summary
Dwarkesh Patel presents a detailed case for moderate short-term bearishness and explosive long-term bullishness on AI progress. [UNVERIFIED] He argues that simply scaling RL atop LLMs will not quickly yield AGI, drawing a parallel to the old expert systems era: instead of hand-coding logic, current labs rely on armies of PhD annotators whose expert reasoning is distilled via behavioral cloning. [UNVERIFIED] Patel uses robotics as a litmus test — human beings flexibly operate new hardware with minimal practice, while current AI requires thousands of handcrafted RL environments per task — and concludes that the "critical core" of generalizable learning is still missing.
