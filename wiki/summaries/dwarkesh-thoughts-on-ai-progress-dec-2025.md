# Thoughts on AI Progress (Dec 2025)
- **Source:** https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/thoughts-on-ai-progress-dec-2025-video
- **Date:** December 2025
- **Author:** Dwarkesh Patel

## Summary
Dwarkesh Patel's December 2025 essay (and accompanying video) argues that the current approach of scaling RL on top of LLMs will not achieve AGI, drawing an explicit parallel to the expert systems era where knowledge was hand-encoded — today we instead pay experts to create instructional data and benchmark tasks through behavioral cloning [UNVERIFIED]. Patel contends that "we still lack the critical core of learning that an actual AGI must possess" and that generalization remains fundamentally unsolved: robots still require vast environment-specific data, and LLMs excel at exams through pattern matching rather than genuine flexible learning [UNVERIFIED]. He is "moderately bearish in the short term" on near-term AGI but "explosively bullish in the long term," expecting nonlinear gains once the right algorithmic breakthroughs are made [UNVERIFIED]. The economic diffusion of current AI systems is characterized as slower and less revolutionary than commonly claimed, because gaps in generalization and learning autonomy prevent widespread automation of human labor [UNVERIFIED].

## Key Claims
- Skepticism about RL on top of LLMs: if we were truly close to a general learner, "mid-training" in fabricated RL environments (spreadsheets, web browsers) would be unnecessary [UNVERIFIED]
- Expert systems parallel: instead of hand-coding rules, we pay experts to create training data — still a form of behavioral cloning, not genuine learning [UNVERIFIED]
- "We still lack the critical core of learning that an actual AGI must possess" [UNVERIFIED]
- Generalization remains unsolved in robotics: one cannot rapidly adapt a robot to new tasks without exhaustive retraining [UNVERIFIED]
- Pre-training may be hitting limits due to data scarcity — scaling alone (bigger models, more data) won't achieve breakthroughs [UNVERIFIED]
- Medium-term bearish, long-term explosively bullish on AI progress [UNVERIFIED]
- Economic and social disruption from AI will lag behind hype until models can learn on the job with little prior task-specific preparation [UNVERIFIED]
