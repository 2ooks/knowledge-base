# 5 AI Stories, 1 Pattern -- xAI, Apple, Davos: Who's Locking In Positions

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/this-week-in-ai-news-xai-got-20b
**Date**: January 24, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Five AI stories revealing a single pattern: structural positions are being locked in and windows are closing. xAI's $20B raise (at ~$230B valuation) cemented that capital flows and safety outcomes are fully decoupled. Apple's Gemini partnership effectively conceded the foundation model race -- if Apple can't build from scratch, nobody else should try. At Davos, Amodei (2026-27 AGI timeline) and Hassabis (end-of-decade, 50% probability) agreed the governance window is closing and junior white-collar roles face near-term displacement. DeepSeek's Engram paper suggests the hardware bottleneck is shifting from GPU compute to system memory, potentially disrupting compute economics.

## Key Claims

- xAI closed $20B Series E at ~$230B valuation; investors include Qatar Investment Authority, Abu Dhabi's MGX, reportedly Nvidia and Cisco
- xAI claims 1M+ H100 GPU equivalents across Colossus I and II; ~600M monthly active users across X and Grok
- xAI secured DoD contract with ceiling up to $200M despite active regulatory investigations over explicit AI-generated imagery
- Apple chose Google Gemini over Anthropic Claude for next-gen Siri; Anthropic "pushed too hard on pricing"
- Amodei argued 2026-27 plausible for extremely capable AGI; some Anthropic engineers now mostly review AI-written code
- DeepSeek Engram architecture: 75-80% capacity for computation (MoE), 20-25% for memory; Needle-in-a-Haystack accuracy jumped from 84.2% to 97%
- Engram enables offloading ~100B parameter embedding tables to system DRAM with minimal throughput penalty
- Kilo Code (GitLab co-founder) launched App Builder after 6-week sprint, targeting engineers rather than non-technical users
