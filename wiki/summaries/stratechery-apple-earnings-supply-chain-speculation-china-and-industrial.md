# Apple Earnings, Supply Chain Speculation, China and Industrial Design

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/apple-earnings-supply-chain-speculation-china-and-industrial-design/
**Date**: February 4, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Apple posted a monster Q1 with iPhone revenue up 23% to $85.3B and record quarterly sales of ~$144B, but was supply-constrained by TSMC's advanced node (3nm) capacity. Thompson speculates that Apple's supply miss reflects a structural shift: AI chip demand from Nvidia, AMD, Microsoft (Maia 200), and Amazon (Trainium 3) is crowding Apple out of its historically privileged position at TSMC. Nvidia has overtaken Apple as TSMC's largest customer. In China, Apple's results confirm Thompson's longstanding thesis that iPhone sales there are driven almost entirely by industrial design changes, with the iPhone 17's new design driving 38% China revenue growth.

## Key Claims

- iPhone revenue grew 23% to $85.3B; total quarterly revenue nearly $144B, both exceeding expectations.
- Apple exited Q4 with "very lean channel inventory" and entered "supply chase mode" due to demand exceeding forecasts.
- Tim Cook explicitly blamed TSMC's 3nm advanced node capacity as the supply constraint, citing it three separate times on the call.
- Nvidia has become TSMC's largest customer, displacing Apple's long-held position.
- AMD MI350, Microsoft Maia 200, and Amazon Trainium 3 are all now competing for TSMC's most advanced process nodes.
- China revenue hit $25.5B, up 38%, with Apple's second-highest China quarter ever.
- Every significant iPhone industrial design change correlates with higher China growth; the iPhone 17 continued this pattern.
- Memory component prices are rising sharply due to AI demand, pressuring Apple's gross margins (guided 48-49% for current quarter).
