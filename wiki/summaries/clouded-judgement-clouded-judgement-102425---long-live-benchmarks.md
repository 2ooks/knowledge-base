# Clouded Judgement 10.24.25 - Long Live Benchmarks

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-102425-long-live
**Date**: October 24, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Argues that traditional model benchmarks (MMLU, HumanEval, GSM8K) have saturated and lost relevance — users now choose models based on trust and experience rather than bar charts, similar to how Google made index-size wars irrelevant for search. But benchmarks are not dead; they are evolving from measuring model capability into becoming the training data itself. Domain-specific rubrics designed by experts now serve as reward functions for reinforcement learning, making the benchmark the teacher rather than the scorecard. The next wave of model differentiation will come from who designs the most meaningful reward functions, not who trains the biggest model.

## Key Claims

- Major models post scores within rounding error on MMLU; users have stopped caring about benchmark charts.
- Parallel drawn to "index wars" in early search — Yahoo, AltaVista, Lycos bragged about pages indexed until Google made it irrelevant.
- Domain-specific benchmarks are becoming reward functions for reinforcement learning — the rubric becomes the training signal.
- Most valuable "labelers" are now domain experts designing benchmarks, not crowdworkers.
- Post-training differentiation will come from custom evaluators: legal models trained on precision/citation rubrics, medical models on caution/accuracy rubrics.
- SaaS median overall multiple: 5.2x; high growth median: 26.8x; median NTM growth rate: 12%.
