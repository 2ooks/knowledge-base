# MacBook Neo, The (Not-So) Thin MacBook, Apple and Memory

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/macbook-neo-the-not-so-thin-macbook-apple-and-memory/
**Date**: March 9, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Apple launched the $599 MacBook Neo using an iPhone A18 Pro chip with 8GB RAM -- its first sub-$1000 Mac laptop and first Mac with a smartphone processor. Thompson argues this is strategically brilliant because cloud-based AI has made local compute less important than ever: a Neo running Claude or ChatGPT has access to more computing power than any local machine. Apple is also cleverly recycling last year's binned A18 Pro chips (some with defective GPUs), making the chip cost potentially near-zero. The timing is perfect -- the "thin client" thesis means the most important computing happens in the cloud, so Apple can massively expand its Mac addressable market with a capable-enough device.

## Key Claims

- MacBook Neo starts at $599, $400 less than any previous new-generation Apple laptop.
- Uses last year's A18 Pro iPhone chip with 8GB RAM (vs. M5 MacBook Air at 16GB RAM starting at $1,099).
- Apple is binning A18 Pro chips: Neo ships with 5 GPUs (vs. 6 in iPhone 16 Pro), using chips with one defective GPU core.
- A-series chips are ~2/3 the size of M-series chips, meaning more chips per TSMC wafer and lower per-chip cost.
- Neo has significantly cheaper components: no Touch-ID on base, smaller battery, no MagSafe, no backlit keyboard, mechanical trackpad, no WiFi 7.
- Asus CFO called the Neo "a shock to the entire market."
- Cloud-based AI makes the Neo's hardware limitations irrelevant for the most compelling computing workloads.
- M5 MacBook Air entry price rose $100 (to $1,099), likely absorbing memory cost increases that the Neo's existence makes viable.
