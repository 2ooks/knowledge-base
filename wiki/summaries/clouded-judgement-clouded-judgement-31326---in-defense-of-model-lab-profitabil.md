# Clouded Judgement 3.13.26 - In Defense of Model Lab Profitability

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-31326-in-defense
**Date**: March 13, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball mounts a detailed defense of AI lab profitability against three bear arguments: structurally low gross margins, unsustainable training costs, and zero switching costs. Drawing parallels to cloud infrastructure companies (Snowflake, Confluent, Mongo), he argues gross margins follow the same early-negative-to-60%+ trajectory, training payback periods are sustainable and improving, and the oligopoly structure (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) mirrors hyperscaler dynamics that support high retention and rational pricing. The strongest counterargument he acknowledges is open-source models cannibalizing the "prototype on lab, deploy on open-source" pipeline.

## Key Claims

- Anthropic reportedly had -94% gross margins in 2024 with a target of ~40% in 2025 -- matching the infra software trajectory.
- Training costs grow 3-5x per model generation, but revenue growth (roughly 2x annually) and expanding revenue bases mean training payback periods are shrinking, not growing.
- Sonnet 3.5 was Anthropic's revenue workhorse for over a year; models have longer commercial lifespans than bears assume.
- The market is consolidating into an oligopoly (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) with dynamics similar to hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP).
- Enterprise switching costs are higher than perceived: fine-tuned models, eval suites, prompt engineering, committed spend contracts, and provider-specific agent ecosystems.
- Open-source models (Llama, DeepSeek, Mistral) keep labs honest on pricing but enterprise customers want SLAs, support, and compliance certifications.
- The "prototype on lab, deploy on open-source" pattern is a canary for the profitability thesis.
- Altimeter is an investor in both OpenAI and Anthropic (disclosed).
