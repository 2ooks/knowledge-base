# METR's Joel Becker on Exponential Time Horizon Evals, Threat Models, and the Limits of AI Productivity

**Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/metr
**Date**: February 27, 2026
**Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

METR's Joel Becker discusses the nuances behind their widely-cited "time horizon" chart that reshaped AGI timeline discourse, including known biases, extrapolation risks, and the difference between exponentials and sigmoids. The conversation also covers METR's lesser-known work on threat evaluation and developer productivity studies, where results were far more mixed than the hype suggests. For BD, this is a critical calibration piece: the benchmarks driving hundreds of billions in AI investment rest on as few as 14 prompts in the 1-4 hour range, and the gap between chart virality and methodological rigor has real implications for partner due diligence.

## Key Claims

- METR's time horizon chart became one of the most influential visualizations in AI, reshaping investment priorities and AGI timelines
- The chart's 1-4 hour task data rests on only ~14 prompts, making extrapolation fragile
- Corrections and nuance disclosures received ~69K views vs. millions of impressions on the original chart
- Developer productivity studies showed Quentin Anthony (Zyphra) as the only productive participant, suggesting AI coding productivity gains are inconsistent
- Exponential vs. sigmoid curve interpretation is a key methodological dispute with massive implications for forecasting
- Compute constraints are identified as a bottleneck slowing progress alongside algorithmic advances
- Prediction markets for model capabilities are discussed as a potential improvement over static benchmarks
