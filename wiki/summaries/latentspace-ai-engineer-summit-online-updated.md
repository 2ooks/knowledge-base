# AI Engineer Summit Online (UPDATED)

**Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/2025-summit-online
**Date**: February 20, 2025
**Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

Latent Space announces the first-ever online track for the 2025 AI Engineer Summit in NYC, adding 30+ talks across 9 hours of content to complement the sold-out in-person event. The talk lineup spans enterprise agent lessons (Cohere, Rasgo), agent reliability and evals (HoneyHive, Root Signals, Traceloop), multi-agent architectures (Best Buy using FSMs), RAG pitfalls (Vectara), voice agents (Fractional), and AI pricing strategy (Orb). The conference structure itself signals that agent engineering has reached sufficient maturity to sustain a dedicated multi-track event with 16:1 CFP selectivity.

## Key Claims

- The AI Engineer Summit sold out its in-person capacity, prompting the first online track with 30+ talks and a 16:1 applicant-to-accepted speaker ratio.
- Talk topics cluster around: enterprise agent deployment lessons, eval frameworks, multi-agent systems, RAG in production, voice agents, and AI pricing.
- Chip Huyen keynoted on agents; Erik Bernhardsson (Modal) keynoted on AI developer experience infrastructure.
- Privacy-first enterprise AI and security-boundary constraints are emerging as distinct talk tracks.
- The "Price of Intelligence" talk by Orb addresses AI agent pricing models for 2025 -- a direct signal on token economics evolution.
- Open model usage patterns explored by Featherless/RWKV in a dedicated talk.
