# Please Stop Forcing Clippy on Those Who Want Anton

**Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/clippy-v-anton
**Date**: April 28, 2025
**Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

Latent Space frames the fundamental product design tension in AI as "Clippy vs. Anton" -- the chatty, personalized companion model versus the concise, efficient tool. The ChatGPT-4o "glazing" incident (where excessive RLHF sycophancy backfired) crystalized this divide, showing that even on the Helpful/Harmless/Honest frontier, there is an unresolvable preference split. Strategically, this maps to a divergence in post-training strategies that is reshaping competitive positioning: OpenAI is increasingly consumer/Clippy-oriented while Anthropic's Claude has pivoted toward developer/Anton use cases, creating distinct market segments for partnership strategy.

## Key Claims

- ChatGPT-4o's April 2025 update produced widely criticized "glazing" behavior attributed to an RLHF overcorrection
- Separate post-training optimizations for chat vs. code use cases now diverge by 123-144 Elo points
- Claude 3.7 Sonnet, ranked 20th on chat leaderboards, significantly outperforms on code benchmarks like Aider
- OpenAI is moving toward a consumer AI company model (premium ChatGPT tiers, planned social network), while Anthropic is becoming a developer-first platform company
- The toggle/personalization approach is described as "an implicit admission that we have yet failed to reach AGI"
- Steven Sinofsky endorses the view: helpfulness decreases as user skill level increases
