# Amazon Earnings, CapEx Concerns, Commodity AI

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/amazon-earnings-capex-concerns-commodity-ai/
**Date**: February 10, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Amazon guided $200B in 2026 capex (nearly 60% YoY increase), sending shares down 9%. Thompson is more cautious on Amazon's AI spending than on Google's or Meta's because Amazon lacks the high-margin 1st-party AI workloads (advertising, productivity software) that justify the others' investments. Amazon's Trainium chip strategy is a long-term bet on AI compute becoming a commodity where profit flows to the lowest-cost provider -- but that bet only pays off when supply meets demand, which is years away. Meanwhile, Amazon's highest-margin business (advertising) is threatened by AI shopping agents that could demote Amazon from Aggregator to aggregated.

## Key Claims

- Amazon guided $200B 2026 capex, ~60% increase YoY, well above Wall Street expectations.
- Big five hyperscalers collectively plan >$700B in 2026 capex, approaching Japan's national budget.
- AWS backlog reached $244B, up 40% YoY and 22% QoQ.
- AWS operating margin was 35% in Q4, up 40 basis points YoY.
- Amazon's projected capex ($200B) exceeds its projected cash from operations ($178B), meaning it will burn cash in 2026.
- Amazon has not bought back stock since 2022 and has never paid a dividend.
- Trainium powers the majority of Amazon's Bedrock service, offering better price-performance than Nvidia chips.
- Amazon has not adopted Google or OpenAI's AI shopping protocols to protect its advertising margin.
