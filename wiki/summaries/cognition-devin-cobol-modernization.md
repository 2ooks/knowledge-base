## Summary
Cognition's Devin agent is being deployed at Fortune 500 companies to automate COBOL modernization, addressing one of enterprise IT's most entrenched infrastructure challenges. Devin has been used to document millions of lines of legacy COBOL code, migrate workflows from COBOL to cloud-native architectures (AWS Lambda), and refactor business logic across hundreds of interdependent modules. The article frames three core challenges: COBOL's data complexity (copybooks, flat memory layouts, inconsistent naming conventions where the same field can have different names across programs), LLMs' lack of COBOL exposure (mainframe code largely absent from training data), and a workforce crisis where 47% of organizations cannot fill COBOL roles and 92% of existing COBOL developers will retire by 2030. Devin's differentiation comes from deep code understanding that preserves business logic during translation, end-to-end autonomous operation in sandboxed environments, and integration with enterprise tools via Microsoft Azure, GitHub, and platforms like Infosys' Topaz Fabric.
