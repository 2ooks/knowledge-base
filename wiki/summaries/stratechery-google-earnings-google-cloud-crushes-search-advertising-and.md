# Google Earnings, Google Cloud Crushes, Search Advertising and LLMs

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/google-earnings-google-cloud-crushes-search-advertising-and-llms/
**Date**: February 9, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Google reported record $403B annual revenue with $132B profit, then guided $175-185B in 2026 capex -- $65.5B above Wall Street expectations. Thompson argues the spend is justified on two fronts: Google Cloud grew 48% YoY to $17.7B with 30% margins while remaining supply-constrained, and LLMs are expanding Google Search's advertising addressable market by understanding long-tail queries and non-English languages that were previously unmonetizable. The latter is the most important finding: LLMs don't just improve ad relevance, they create entirely new ad inventory -- and Google's side project in conversational AI ads (its "meh" AI Mode experiment) is essentially OpenAI's entire proposed business.

## Key Claims

- Google reported $403B revenue in 2025 (first time above $400B) and $132B profit.
- 2026 capex guided at $175-185B, up from $91B in 2025; the top end was $65.5B above Wall Street expectations.
- Google Cloud grew 48% YoY in Q4 to $17.7B revenue with 30% operating margin (best ever).
- Google Cloud backlog increased 55% sequentially and more than doubled YoY, reaching $240B.
- Gemini models improve Search query understanding at a rate of nearly one launch per month for two years.
- LLMs enable Google to monetize longer, more complex searches previously impossible to match with ads -- creating new inventory.
- LLMs significantly improve non-English language query understanding, expanding global ad market.
- In Q4 alone, advertisers used Gemini to create nearly 70 million creative assets via text customization.
- Nearly 1 in 6 AI Mode queries are now non-text (voice or images).
- Pichai: Google expects to remain supply-constrained through 2026.
