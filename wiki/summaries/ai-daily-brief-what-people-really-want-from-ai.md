# What People Really Want From AI

**Source URL**: https://aidailybrief.beehiiv.com/p/what-people-really-want-from-ai
**Date**: March 19, 2026
**Publication**: AI Daily Brief

## Summary

Analyzes Anthropic's 81,000-person qualitative study across 159 countries, the largest ever conducted, using Claude as interviewer. The core finding is that hope and alarm coexist within individuals rather than dividing into camps — people want AI for professional excellence, personal transformation, and time freedom, while fearing unreliability, job loss, and cognitive atrophy. Economic benefits accrue disproportionately to independent workers and entrepreneurs, while freelance creatives are the most exposed middle. Microsoft restructured Copilot under a single leader (Jacob Andreou) while Suleyman pivots to model training, signaling that separated consumer/commercial AI organizations are failing.

## Key Claims

- Anthropic surveyed 81,000 people across 159 countries and 70 languages using Claude as the interviewer.
- Top hope: professional excellence (18.8%). Top fear: unreliability (26.7%).
- 91% of those citing learning benefits had experienced them, vs. only 46% of those fearing cognitive atrophy who had seen it firsthand.
- Employees with side projects reported economic gains at 58% — the highest of any group.
- Copyright concerns only 4%, environmental costs 4%, existential risk 6.7% of responses — far below media coverage emphasis.
- Microsoft combined consumer and commercial Copilot teams under Jacob Andreou (EVP), freeing Suleyman for model training.
- Claude Cowork Dispatch updated to support Claude Code sessions — Cowork and Claude Code converging on same primitives.
