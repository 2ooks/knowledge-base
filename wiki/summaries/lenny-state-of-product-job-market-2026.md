# State of the Product Job Market in 2026
- **Source:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/state-of-the-product-job-market-in-ee9
- **Date:** ~April 2026
- **Author:** Lenny Rachitsky (Lenny's Newsletter), with data from TrueUp

## Summary
The fourth biannual "State of the Product Job Market" report from Lenny's Newsletter delivers a surprisingly optimistic picture of tech hiring despite ongoing layoff headlines. Based on TrueUp data tracking 9,000+ tech companies, PM openings are at their highest in 3+ years (7,300+ globally, up 75% from early 2023 lows and 20% since the start of 2026), engineering openings exceed 67,000 globally (26,000 in the US alone), and AI roles are "hockey-sticking." However, design roles have plateaued at ~5,700 globally since early 2023, and the PM-to-designer demand ratio has flipped from favoring designers to a 1.27x PM advantage — a potential signal that AI-driven engineering velocity is reducing the need for traditional design processes. Remote work continues to decline while the Bay Area strengthens its concentration, now accounting for 23% of PM roles and a third of all AI roles.

## Key Claims
- 7,300+ open PM roles at tech companies globally — highest in 3+ years, 75% above early 2023 low, up ~20% since start of 2026
- 67,000+ open engineering roles globally (26,000 in US) — growth accelerating
- AI roles are "hockey-sticking" — includes all roles at AI companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cursor, Lovable) plus AI-specific roles at non-AI companies
- Demand for AI engineers and AI PMs is similarly exploding
- Tech recruiter openings near 2022 peak levels — leading indicator of sustained hiring demand
- Design roles plateaued at ~5,700 globally since early 2023 — potentially AI-related
- PM-to-designer demand ratio flipped in mid-2023; now 1.27x favoring PMs
- 23% of open PM roles are in the Bay Area (up 50% since 2022); 20%+ of eng/design roles
- One-third of open AI roles are Bay Area-based (flat share, but absolute numbers growing)
- NYC established as #2 tech jobs location globally; Bengaluru, London, Tel Aviv, Singapore as top international hubs
- Remote work opportunities continue to decline across all tech roles
- Despite ongoing layoffs, overall number of tech jobs continues to grow
- Data sourced from TrueUp, tracking 9,000+ tech companies and top startups worldwide
