# Clouded Judgement 1.2.26 - Authority Is the AI Bottleneck

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-1226-authority
**Date**: January 2, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Identifies the key bottleneck in enterprise AI adoption as not model capability or cost but authority — whether organizations allow AI to be authoritative (act and own outcomes) versus merely assistive (suggest and wait for human approval). Most enterprise AI remains assistive, producing marginal efficiency gains without step-change outcomes. Companies seeing outsized returns pick narrow domains, define tight guardrails, invest in sources of truth, and then let the system act. The analogy to cloud trust adoption is direct: it took years for enterprises to trust cloud infrastructure, and the same trust-building curve applies to authoritative AI.

## Key Claims

- Most enterprise AI is firmly assistive — AI drafts but humans send, AI suggests but humans approve, AI summarizes but humans act.
- Assistive AI improves efficiency at the margin; authoritative AI rewrites the workflow and enables cost structure changes.
- Teams with outsized AI returns are not using more advanced models — they are explicitly deciding where software takes responsibility.
- Authoritative AI forces hard organizational questions: canonical sources of truth, acceptable error rates, accountability for automated decisions, rollback mechanisms.
- Trust in AI systems will follow the same adoption curve as trust in cloud infrastructure — slow buildup followed by floodgates.
- SaaS median overall multiple: 4.7x; high growth median: 14.6x; median NTM growth rate: 12%.
- Median SaaS FCF margin: 19%; median CAC payback: 36 months.
