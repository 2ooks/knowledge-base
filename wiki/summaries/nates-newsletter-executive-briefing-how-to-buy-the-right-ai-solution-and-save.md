# Executive Briefing: How to Buy The Right AI Solution and Save Millions

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/executive-briefing-how-to-buy-the
**Date**: September 21, 2025
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Addresses the gap between rising AI spend ($1.2M average per company in 2025) and executives' inability to evaluate AI vendors, which creates a fertile market for scams and regretted purchases. Frames AI products as "living systems" requiring new operational paradigms including observability, versioning, and model drift management -- fundamentally different from traditional software procurement. Argues that delegating AI purchasing to IT is mistake number one, and provides a due diligence playbook to avoid the vendor failures that drove the "95% of AI Projects Fail" narrative from MIT interviews.

## Key Claims

- Average company AI spend is $1.2 million in 2025 and rising fast.
- Bad AI purchases were cited by many executives in MIT interviews that led to the "95% of AI Projects Fail" headline.
- AI products are living systems requiring observability, versioning, and model drift management -- unlike traditional software.
- Delegating AI purchase decisions to IT alone is identified as mistake number one.
- A cottage industry of companies has emerged in 2025 focused solely on cleaning up messes left by bad AI vendors.
- AI-specific due diligence requires different practices than traditional software procurement.
- The vendor risk is not just wasted spend but months of delay, competitor momentum, and team morale damage.
