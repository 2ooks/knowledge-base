# 20 Hours of News in 10 Minutes: The Week AI Stopped Being a Thing Companies Talk About

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/135-billion-in-capex-a-robotaxi-in
**Date**: January 31, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

A five-story weekly roundup analyzing the moment AI shifted from corporate aspiration to operational commitment. The Meta-Microsoft earnings divergence (both beat expectations; Meta +10%, Microsoft -10%) crystallized the market's verdict: owning your AI capabilities beats renting them. Meanwhile, Tesla announced discontinuation of Model S/X to convert Fremont lines to Optimus robot production, and OpenClaw crossed 100K GitHub stars in a week, signaling explosive demand for open-source autonomous agents. The through-line is irreversible commitment -- capital, product, and strategic decisions that cannot be unwound cheaply.

## Key Claims

- Meta guided 2026 CapEx at $115-135B (nearly doubling from $72B in 2025); stock rose 10%
- Microsoft disclosed 45% of its $625B commercial backlog is tied to OpenAI commitments; stock dropped ~$350B in value
- Azure grew 39% (down from 40% prior quarter) -- one point of deceleration triggered massive repricing
- Nvidia invested $2B in CoreWeave targeting 5GW of AI data center capacity by 2030
- Microsoft signed a $750M three-year Foundry deal with Perplexity; 1,500+ Foundry customers already mixing OpenAI and Anthropic models
- Tesla discontinuing Model S/X to produce 1M Optimus robots/year; invested $2B in xAI; 1.1M active FSD subscriptions (+38% YoY)
- OpenClaw crossed 100K GitHub stars and 2M visitors in 7 days
- Anthropic closed funding above $10B target at $350B valuation (~double its September valuation)
