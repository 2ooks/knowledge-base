# Ads in ChatGPT, Why OpenAI Needs Ads, The Long Road to Instagram

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/ads-in-chatgpt-why-openai-needs-ads-the-long-road-to-instagram/
**Date**: January 20, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

OpenAI is introducing contextual ads in ChatGPT's free and Go tiers, marking a strategic reversal from Altman's previous anti-advertising stance. Thompson argues this is existentially necessary because OpenAI's costs scale linearly with revenue (compute grew 3x YoY tracking revenue 3x YoY), making advertising the only business model that can bend the revenue curve above the cost curve. The initial contextual ad product is inferior to what OpenAI ultimately needs -- a Meta-like personalized ad engine -- and the company faces a timing disadvantage against Google, which can subsidize Gemini indefinitely with its existing search ad business.

## Key Claims

- OpenAI's compute grew from 0.2 GW (2023) to 0.6 GW (2024) to ~1.9 GW (2025), with revenue tracking the same curve: $2B to $6B to $20B+ ARR.
- Epoch AI estimated 70% of OpenAI's 2024 compute costs went to R&D, not inference.
- OpenAI committed ~$1.4 trillion in data center and chip spending.
- OpenAI's CFO Sarah Frier admitted revenue directly tracks available compute, meaning the company lacks a scalable business model.
- Initial ads will be contextual, shown at the bottom of responses for free/Go tier users in the US.
- OpenAI needs to evolve toward a Meta-like personalized ad model that targets users independent of conversation context.
- Google can subsidize Gemini indefinitely via Search ads, creating an existential competitive threat if OpenAI degrades UX with ads.
