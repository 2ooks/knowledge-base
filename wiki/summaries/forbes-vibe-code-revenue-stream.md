# How to Vibe Code a New Revenue Stream Using What You Already Know

**Source URL:** https://www.forbes.com/sites/jodiecook/2026/04/05/how-to-vibe-code-a-new-revenue-stream-using-what-you-already-know/
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Author:** Jodie Cook

## Summary
"Vibe coding" using AI tools like Claude Code enables non-technical founders to transform domain expertise into live, deployable software products by describing functionality in natural language, compressing development timelines from months to days or weeks. Evan G. states he can "do in a day what used to take a month" and simultaneously builds patty.com, brooke.com, and racingminds.com without writing code. This speed change fundamentally alters the economics of product experimentation—reducing the cost and risk of testing ideas to "almost nothing"—while allowing founders to leverage existing audiences, customer insights, and refined frameworks to create new revenue streams.

## Key Claims
- Evan G., software builder and founder, states: "I live in Claude Code every day building software at a rate that is just insane. I can do in a day what used to take a month," simultaneously building patty.com, brooke.com, and racingminds.com without writing traditional code
- Arthur Kerekes ("100% Claudepilled over here. My entire life and business is run from the command line") launched multiple production systems in "a few months": bananacam.ai (AI photo booth with 20,000 styles and subscriptions), contentsidekick.ai (cross-platform content pipeline with nine specialized agents), LinkedIn outreach automation, and Gmail marketing system generating "hundreds of personalised email drafts"
- Dhanyal Radha Gopi used Claude to compile 30-day video marathon performance data (YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn) into a public insights page, then fed the analytics back to Claude to generate platform-specific videos optimized for data-driven goals
- Chad Thomas is building a "gstack framework that applies to product management" using Claude, shipping the framework as a live product rather than leaving it as documentation
- The article frames vibe coding as "compressing the timeline between having an idea and having something live" and states the "cost of being wrong dropped to almost nothing" for product testing
- Founders are advised to "load your ICP document and brand guidelines at the start of every session" and leverage existing audience feedback to identify monetizable products
- The article recommends: "Take the one [framework] you explain most often and build the version someone can use without you in the room. Ship it to the ten people most likely to find it useful."
- Wispr Flow mentioned as a tool for dictating prompts to create testable product versions "by the end of the week"
- Claude Cowork mentioned as a tool for uploading content performance data to extract audience insights that "shape the product, the format, and the platform"
