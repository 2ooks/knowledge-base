# The Scientist and the Simulator

**Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/scientist-simulator
**Date**: February 10, 2026
**Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

Melissa Du introduces a taxonomy for AI-driven scientific discovery that separates "scientists" (LLMs for reasoning/synthesis) from "simulators" (domain-specific ML models for physical prediction), arguing they require distinct talent, data infrastructure, and investment. The piece makes a compelling case that frontier labs like OpenAI and Anthropic will not solve disease or materials by scaling LLMs alone -- domain-specific simulators trained on physics-based and wet-lab data are the actual bottleneck. For BD, this reframes the AI-for-science investment landscape: the ~$111B flowing to foundation model companies vastly outpaces ~$7.6B in AI drug discovery and ~$500M-$1B in materials/climate, creating a massive underfunded opportunity.

## Key Claims

- AI foundation model companies raised ~$111B in 2024-2025 ($31B + $80B), while AI drug discovery totaled ~$7.6B and materials/climate ~$500M-$1B
- OpenAI and Anthropic captured nearly 14% of all global VC in 2025
- Over 90% of drugs that work in mice fail in human trials, highlighting the simulation gap
- GraphCast (Google DeepMind) exceeded 50 years of physics-based weather prediction accuracy in under a minute on a single TPU
- AlphaFold2 predicted structures for 200M+ proteins; GNoME discovered 2.2M new crystal structures
- None of these science breakthroughs used transformer architectures -- GraphCast is a GNN, DNA models use hybrid SSM architectures
- 66% of physicians use AI in clinic; 47% of developers use AI coding assistants daily
- Biology lacks the elegant compression of physics -- the genome's 3B base pairs and 130K-650K protein-protein interaction types resist averaging
