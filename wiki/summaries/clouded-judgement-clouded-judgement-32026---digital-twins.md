# Clouded Judgement 3.20.26 - Digital Twins

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-32026-digital-twins
**Date**: March 20, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball identifies digital twins as a rapidly emerging concept in the agent space -- not the manufacturing kind, but AI-era knowledge representations that bridge tacit human expertise to agent-consumable formats. He outlines five flavors: workflow knowledge capture, institutional memory retention, expert twins (scaling top performers), customer twins (queryable customer intelligence), and personal monetization (individuals packaging their expertise as scalable digital products). The core thesis is that the bottleneck to the agentic era is not model intelligence but knowledge representation.

## Key Claims

- Digital twins are appearing as a recurring pattern across agent-space founder pitches -- a leading indicator of category formation.
- The bottleneck to the agentic era is knowledge representation, not model intelligence; agents can only act on knowledge they have access to.
- Five flavors identified: workflow capture, institutional memory, expert twins, customer twins, and personal monetization.
- Expert twins address the "power law of talent" problem -- one top performer's judgment becomes accessible to the entire org.
- Customer twins (companies like Simile, Aru) enable unlimited synthetic research studies against a digital representation of your customer base.
- The job displacement question reframes from "will AI take my job?" to "can I build a digital twin of myself before someone else does it for me?"
- Edra launched a product focused on workflow knowledge capture for agent automation.
