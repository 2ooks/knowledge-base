# The Race to Put AI Agents Everywhere

**Source URL**: https://aidailybrief.beehiiv.com/p/the-race-to-put-ai-agents-everywhere
**Date**: March 18, 2026
**Publication**: AI Daily Brief

## Summary

Covers the Q2 2026 sprint to productize AI agents for enterprise, anchored by NVIDIA's GTC announcements. NVIDIA launched NemoClaw — sandboxed, policy-based security for OpenClaw — positioning it as the enterprise-readiness layer the agent ecosystem lacked. Jensen Huang explicitly compared OpenClaw to Linux and Kubernetes in terms of platform significance. Meanwhile, OpenAI is pivoting hard away from its "series of startups" model toward laser focus on enterprise coding (Codex subagents), while Meta's Manus launches local desktop agent access and Adaptive ships always-on agent computers for small businesses. The competitive landscape is splitting between agent consolidation (OpenAI) and agent extensibility (Anthropic).

## Key Claims

- Jensen Huang projects $1 trillion in NVIDIA revenue through 2027, doubling his prior $500B forecast for 2026 alone.
- Meta signed a $27B five-year infrastructure deal with Nebius (neocloud), on top of a prior $3B agreement.
- NVIDIA's NemoClaw adds sandboxed execution, policy-based security, and formalized access controls to OpenClaw — model and hardware agnostic.
- GPT-5.4 hit an annualized run rate of $1B in net-new API revenue within one week of launch.
- OpenAI restructured Stargate under former Intel exec Sachin Katti, now willing to lease rather than own data centers.
- Alibaba restructured AI into "Alibaba Token Hub" with explicit mandate to "create tokens, deliver tokens, and apply tokens."
- Chinese AI labs keeping strategic agent-focused models proprietary while releasing lightweight open-source models for distribution.
