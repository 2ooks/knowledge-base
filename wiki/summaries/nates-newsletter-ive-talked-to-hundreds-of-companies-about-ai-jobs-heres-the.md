# I've Talked to Hundreds of Companies About AI & Jobs: Here's The Jobs Guide No One is Writing

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/the-scoop-what-companies-tell-me
**Date**: November 4, 2025
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter (Substack)

## Summary

Based on closed-door conversations with Fortune 100 leaders and scale-up executives, the article reveals a widening gap between public corporate messaging on AI ("upskilling everyone") and private strategy sessions asking "who can we replace?" It maps three distinct career-level strategies: juniors face a Value Visibility Gap where production work is AI-replaceable, mid-career professionals should double down on domain expertise as their moat, and seniors enjoy a Grace Period where companies relax AI proficiency requirements in exchange for systems thinking. The framework matters for BD because it signals how enterprise buyers are actually restructuring talent around AI -- shaping demand for tools that amplify domain expertise vs. replace production work.

## Key Claims

- Companies publicly say "AI transformation" while privately asking "who can we replace? who's demonstrating they can do more with AI?"
- Juniors (0-5 years) face a Value Visibility Gap: companies cannot distinguish their output from what ChatGPT produces.
- Mid-career professionals (5-10 years) should protect domain expertise -- AI commoditizes skills but cannot replicate accumulated domain knowledge.
- Seniors (10+ years) have a Grace Period: companies are actively reducing AI assessment requirements for senior hires.
- OpenAI itself hires junior "vibe coders" for creative AI experimentation alongside super-senior engineers for systems thinking.
- The best organizations rebuild career ladders to assess problem-solving ability at every level, not production output.
- Skills like sharing prompts, teaching AI decomposition, and building reusable frameworks are now baseline expectations for mid-career professionals.
