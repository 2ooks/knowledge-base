# Hiring Scouts to Help Me Find Guests

**Source URL**: https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/hiring-scouts-to-help-me-find-guests
**Date**: January 15, 2026
**Publication**: Dwarkesh Patel

## Summary

Patel posts a hiring call for part-time research scouts ($100/hr) to identify polymathic guests across bio, history, econ, math/physics, and AI/hardware. The substantive value is in the embedded research questions, which reveal frontier thinking on RL scaling, drug discovery bottlenecks, AI-for-math limitations, and what explosive economic growth from AI would actually look like materially. For BD purposes, the open questions on RL training efficiency, transfer learning opacity, and whether automated formal math unlocks downstream engineering progress are direct signals of where the next wave of infrastructure and tooling demand will emerge.

## Key Claims

- RL progress over the past year partly results from going from near-zero to ~1e26 FLOPs training compute, making scaling exponents still uncertain.
- It is unclear how much RL progress comes from inference scaling (which has worse variable economics) vs. training improvements.
- Despite 1,000,000x reduction in genome sequencing costs over 30 years, drug development rates have not accelerated and may be slowing.
- Transfer learning and meta-learning from RL training remain opaque -- even lab insiders cannot or will not share concrete details.
- AI-for-math may be limited if mathematics is dialectical concept construction rather than proof search within fixed formal systems.
- Patel seeks Karpathy-type figures: expert-level, no vested interest in a specific company, willing to give unbiased technical assessments.
