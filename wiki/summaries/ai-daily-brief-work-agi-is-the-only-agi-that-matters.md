# Work AGI Is the Only AGI That Matters

**Source URL**: https://aidailybrief.beehiiv.com/p/work-agi-is-the-only-agi-that-matters
**Date**: March 26, 2026
**Publication**: AI Daily Brief

## Summary

OpenAI executed its most dramatic strategic pivot yet: renaming its product division "AGI Deployment," discontinuing Sora, shelving Instant Checkout, and concentrating on Work AGI — the ability to automate long chains of knowledge work tasks with minimal human oversight. Sam Altman narrowed his role to capital, supply chains, and data centers while Fidji Simo takes operational control. A new model codenamed "Spud" has completed pretraining. Jensen Huang declared "we've achieved AGI" at task-level but acknowledged 100,000 agents could not build NVIDIA. The practical framing: task-level AGI exists today; Work AGI — sustained autonomous execution across chained tasks — is the actual frontier.

## Key Claims

- OpenAI renamed its product division to "AGI Deployment" and discontinued Sora entirely, freeing compute for model Spud.
- Sam Altman stepped back from day-to-day commercial strategy; Fidji Simo effectively runs operations.
- OpenAI's Sora shutdown ended a billion-dollar Disney partnership; freed compute redirected to Spud.
- OpenAI seeking additional $10B on top of $110B already raised; risk disclosure flags heavy Microsoft reliance.
- SpaceX could file for the largest IPO in history ($75B target) as soon as this week, with xAI first major AI startup to go public.
- Fundrise AI ETF (holding SpaceX, Anthropic, OpenAI) trading at 1,200% premium to NAV — implied Anthropic valuation near $5T.
- SoftBank committed another $30B to OpenAI, testing its self-imposed 25% loan-to-value limit; stock down 45%.
- Federal judge called Pentagon's treatment of Anthropic "troubling," suggesting First Amendment violation.
- Jensen Huang: "I think we've achieved AGI" — but clarified the odds of 100,000 agents building NVIDIA is "zero percent."
