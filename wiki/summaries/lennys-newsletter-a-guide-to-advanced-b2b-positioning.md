# A Guide to Advanced B2B Positioning

**Source URL**: https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/a-guide-to-advanced-b2b-positioning
**Date**: March 10, 2026
**Publication**: Lenny's Newsletter

## Summary

April Dunford, author of Obviously Awesome, identifies four non-obvious roadblocks that derail B2B positioning exercises: cross-functional disagreement about competitive alternatives, product pessimism that blinds teams to strengths, poorly defined differentiated value, and confusion about what exactly is being positioned. The piece argues that as AI makes building and launching products trivial, distribution and positioning become the primary competitive moat. For BD, this is directly actionable: the framework for resolving internal disagreements about competitive framing, anchoring positioning in the prospect's view, and avoiding feature-level value articulation applies to how Microsoft positions GitHub Copilot, Azure AI, and partner solutions against an increasingly crowded landscape.

## Key Claims

- AI is making product building trivial, shifting the biggest challenge to distribution and positioning
- B2B vendors lose ~50% of sales opportunities to the status quo ("no decision"), not named competitors
- Cross-functional teams (marketing, product, sales, founder) consistently disagree on who they compete with, often the root cause of weak positioning
- Marketing overweights competitor visibility; Product tracks "horizon competitors" never seen in deals; Sales has "whale bias" from losing single large deals
- Differentiated value must answer "why pick us over alternatives?" -- not list features, not abstract to "saves money"
- Product pessimism is contagious and prevents teams from positioning around actual competitive strengths
- Founders can muddy positioning when biased by outdated competitive landscapes or fundraising-oriented future-state framing
- April Dunford has worked with 300+ B2B companies on positioning over 10 years
