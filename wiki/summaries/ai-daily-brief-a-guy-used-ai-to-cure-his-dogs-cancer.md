# A Guy Used AI To Cure His Dog's Cancer

**Source URL**: https://aidailybrief.beehiiv.com/p/a-guy-used-ai-to-cure-his-dog-s-cancer
**Date**: March 16, 2026
**Publication**: AI Daily Brief

## Summary

Frames the current AI discourse as "AI's second moment" — analogous to the ChatGPT launch but with dramatically higher stakes, broader participation, and real economic consequences. The viral story of a layperson using ChatGPT and AlphaFold to develop a personalized mRNA cancer vaccine for a dog illustrates how AI enables non-experts to navigate complex scientific pipelines, while the misinterpretation of Karpathy's job exposure scores reveals the persistent gap between AI exposure and actual displacement. Economists and researchers argue that high AI exposure may actually increase hiring and wages in affected occupations, complicating the dominant doom narrative.

## Key Claims

- 27 firms have listed AI agents as a material risk in SEC filings in 2026, up from 7 at the same point last year.
- NVIDIA GTC 2026 featured Groq-powered inference servers — NVIDIA's first chip manufactured outside TSMC, using Samsung's foundry.
- Karpathy's job treemap scored 342 BLS occupations on AI exposure; average score was 5.3, software devs scored 8-9.
- Economist Kevin Bryan offered to bet $1,000 that most "susceptible" jobs see increased labor share by 2030.
- ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott predicted college grad unemployment could reach mid-30s percent within a couple of years; current underemployment is already at 42.5%.
- Former Anthropic researchers raising $175M at $1B valuation for Mirendil (AI-enhanced scientific research).
