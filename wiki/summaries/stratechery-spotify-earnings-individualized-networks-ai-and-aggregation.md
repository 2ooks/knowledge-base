# Spotify Earnings, Individualized Networks, AI and Aggregation

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/spotify-earnings-individualized-networks-ai-and-aggregation/
**Date**: February 11, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Spotify reported record user growth (751M MAU, 290M paid) and outgoing CEO Daniel Ek framed the company's moat as technology that makes every user's experience uniquely individualized -- the same trick every successful network business uses. New co-CEO Gustav Soderström argued AI is a sustaining technology for Spotify's aggregator position: it strengthens personalization, generates a proprietary language-to-music dataset no LLM can commoditize, and increases Spotify's power over supply as AI-generated content floods the market. The broader claim is that in periods of lower friction, markets aggregate rather than disaggregate -- directly countering the thesis that AI will unbundle SaaS and media incumbents.

## Key Claims

- Spotify added 38M new listeners in Q4 2025, reaching 751M MAU and 290M paid subscribers -- a record quarter.
- ~90M subscribers have used Interactive DJ, driving over 4B hours of listening time.
- Soderström argues real disruption comes from new business models, not new technology alone -- and Spotify's ads-plus-subscription model remains the dominant consumer paradigm.
- Spotify is building a proprietary "language-to-music" dataset from hundreds of millions of users that cannot be replicated by generic LLMs because music preference is subjective and personal, not factual.
- AI-generated music flooding the supply side actually increases Spotify's power as the gatekeeper that decides what breaks -- charts and cultural moments happen on Spotify.
- In times of lower friction, markets tend to aggregate, not disaggregate -- counter to the "everyone rolls their own" fear about AI disrupting software companies.
- Marginal cost of inference will approach zero for scaled content services, preserving the unified content business model (ads + subscription).
