# The Real Reason Amazon Cut 30,000 People + What the Balance Sheet Actually Shows

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/amazon-isnt-replacing-workers-with
**Date**: January 30, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Amazon's 30,000-person layoff is not primarily about culture or AI replacing workers -- it's capital reallocation. With quarterly free cash flow going negative (-$4.8B) while CapEx hit $125B (75% to AI infrastructure), Amazon is converting human headcount to compute capacity. The 30,000 cuts save ~$6B/year, roughly the cost of Project Rainier (500,000 Trainium2 chips). The "culture" narrative from CEO Jassy is carefully constructed to satisfy three audiences (employees, investors, regulators) while obscuring the balance sheet reality. For Microsoft BD, this is the clearest signal yet that the hyperscaler arms race is structural: aggregate CapEx among the big five now consumes 94% of operating cash flows, and every company is running the same arithmetic -- human capital out, compute capital in.

## Key Claims

- Amazon CapEx hit $125B in 2025 (up 61% YoY), the highest of any company on Earth; 75% goes directly to AI infrastructure.
- Quarterly free cash flow went negative at -$4.8B; trailing twelve-month FCF dropped 61% YoY; FCF margin collapsed from 8.73% to 2.7%.
- Amazon raised $12B in debt specifically to fund AI infrastructure investments.
- 30,000 cuts at ~$200K median total comp = ~$6B/year in savings, roughly equal to Project Rainier's cost.
- Goldman Sachs projects top hyperscalers will spend $1.15 trillion on infrastructure between 2025-2027, more than double the prior three years combined.
- Aggregate CapEx among big five hyperscalers now consumes 94% of operating cash flows after dividends and buybacks.
- Amazon corporate headcount tripled between 2017 and 2022; the correction that started in 2023 is not over.
- Jassy's own June 2025 memo warned AI would mean "fewer people doing some of the jobs being done today" -- then October earnings call claimed cuts were "not AI-driven."
