# AINews: H100 Prices Are Melting *UP*

- **Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-h100-prices-are-melting-up
- **Date**: March 28, 2026
- **Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

H100 rental prices have reversed their 2024 depreciation trend and risen sharply since December 2025, driven by the reasoning model/agent inflection, a general chip shortage, and dramatically improved inference software that makes the 4-year-old chip more valuable than initial 4-7 year depreciation schedules assumed. This has meaningful implications for data center business models and GPU economics — the chip is now worth more than when it shipped. The broader AINews covers the leaked Anthropic "Capybara" tier above Opus (reported gains on coding, academic reasoning, and cybersecurity), GLM-5.1 narrowing the open-vs-closed coding model gap (scoring 45.3 vs Claude Opus 4.6's 47.9), Hermes Agent emerging as the open-agent focal point with Hugging Face integration of 28 curated models, and TurboQuant/RotorQuant innovations enabling Qwen 3.5 9B on a base MacBook Air with 20K context.

## Key Claims

- H100 rental prices have risen sharply since December 2025, reversing the 2024 depreciation cycle
- H100s are now worth more than 3 years ago, per Dylan on Dwarkesh podcast
- The price reversal is driven by reasoning model/agent inflection (December 2025), general chip shortage, and improved inference software
- Initial 4-7 year GPU depreciation schedules are being invalidated by rising chip utility
- Anthropic leaked "Capybara" tier above Opus, described as "larger and more intelligent" than Claude Opus 4.6, with gains on coding, reasoning, and cybersecurity
- Google reportedly close to funding Anthropic's data center (Financial Times)
- GLM-5.1 scores 45.3 on coding eval vs Claude Opus 4.6 at 47.9, showing a narrowing open-vs-closed gap
- Hermes Agent integrated Hugging Face as a first-class inference provider with 28 curated models
- RotorQuant achieves 10-19x speed improvement over TurboQuant with 44x fewer parameters using Clifford Algebra
- TurboQuant KV dequant optimization yields +22.8% decode speed at 32K context by skipping 90% of dequant work
- Qwen 3.5 9B runs on a base MacBook Air M4 16GB with 20K context via TurboQuant
