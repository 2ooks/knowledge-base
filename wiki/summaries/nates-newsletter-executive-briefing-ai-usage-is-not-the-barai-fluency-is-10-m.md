# Executive Briefing: AI Usage Is Not the Bar -- AI Fluency Is 10x More Valuable

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/executive-briefing-ai-usage-is-not
**Date**: October 19, 2025
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Based on 2,000 hours observing AI-enabled teams, the author identifies a 10x gap between teams with AI "usage" (30% productivity gains) and teams with AI "fluency" (300% gains). The difference is not tool knowledge but three root principles: constraints over process, AI-shaped problem-solving skills (five components), and deliberately avoiding premature infrastructure. The piece argues that 90% of AI projects remain stuck in POC purgatory because organizations train on tools rather than building judgment that compounds, and that small fluent teams routinely outperform large trained ones.

## Key Claims

- AI fluent teams achieve 300% productivity gains vs. 30% for teams that merely "use" AI -- a 10x difference.
- 10 people with AI fluency can outperform 500 people with AI training.
- Three root principles drive organizational AI fluency: constraints over process, AI-shaped problem-solving skills, and no premature infrastructure.
- The five transferable AI problem-solving skills are observable across high-performing teams regardless of domain.
- 90% of AI projects remain stuck in POC purgatory (implied from scoring context on RL training and realistic environments).
- Fluency cannot be taught by teaching a tool like ChatGPT; it requires building judgment that compounds.
- The key diagnostic question: "Are you building capability that lets you tackle harder problems, or just getting faster at the easy ones?"
