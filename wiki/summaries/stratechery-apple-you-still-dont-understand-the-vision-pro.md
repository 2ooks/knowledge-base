# Apple: You (Still) Don't Understand the Vision Pro

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/apple-you-still-dont-understand-the-vision-pro/
**Date**: January 12, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Thompson critiques Apple's fundamental misunderstanding of the Vision Pro's value proposition through its first live NBA broadcast. Apple produced the game like traditional TV -- multiple camera angles, dedicated announcers, a studio show -- when the Vision Pro's entire point is immersive presence, not produced content. Thompson argues Apple should use a single fixed camera and no production, because the constant camera switching destroys the sense of "being there" that makes the device worth $3,499. The piece is a case study in how legacy content production paradigms undermine new technology platforms, and why Apple's content strategy -- not hardware -- is the Vision Pro's biggest bottleneck.

## Key Claims

- Apple's first live NBA game on Vision Pro (Bucks vs. Lakers) used multiple cameras with constant cuts, destroying immersion.
- Fox used 147 cameras for the last Super Bowl broadcast; the entire TV production paradigm is designed for 2D screens.
- Only 6 total Lakers games are scheduled for Vision Pro this season, partly because the costly production model limits content volume.
- Thompson argues one fixed camera with no production would be both cheaper and create a superior experience.
- The MLS Season in Review video had 54 distinct shots in 5 minutes -- an average of one cut every 6 seconds.
- Vision Pro cameras should be standard equipment at every stadium and concert venue worldwide.
- The M5 Vision Pro hardware is capable but Apple's content production undermines the device's unique value.
