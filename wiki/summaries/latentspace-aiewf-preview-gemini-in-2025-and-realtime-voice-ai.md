# [AIEWF Preview] Gemini in 2025 and Realtime Voice AI

**Source URL**: https://www.latent.space/p/aiewf-preview-gemini-in-2025-and
**Date**: June 2, 2025
**Publication**: Latent Space

## Summary

Cross-podcast preview recorded at Google I/O with Sam Charrington (TWiML AI), featuring Logan Kilpatrick (Google/Gemini), Shrestha Basu Mallick, and Kwindla Hultman Kramer (Pipecat) discussing the Gemini Live API and the state of real-time voice AI ahead of AIEWF 2025. The episode signals that real-time voice is moving from demo to production, with dedicated workshops on millisecond-latency workflows using Gemini Live API and Pipecat. For BD, voice agents represent a new consumption surface that could drive significant API/compute demand as enterprises move from text-based to multimodal agent interactions.

## Key Claims

- Google I/O featured the Gemini Live API as a production-ready real-time voice interface, not just a demo.
- Pipecat is positioned as the open-source framework for building voice agents on top of Gemini and other model APIs.
- AIEWF 2025 dedicated multiple workshop and talk slots specifically to real-time voice AI, indicating maturation of the category.
- The "milliseconds to magic" framing emphasizes that latency is the critical UX threshold for voice agent adoption.
- Voice AI is treated as a distinct engineering discipline requiring its own infrastructure stack (not just a wrapper on text APIs).
