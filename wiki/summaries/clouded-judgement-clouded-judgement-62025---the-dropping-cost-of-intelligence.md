# Clouded Judgement 6.20.25 - The Dropping Cost of Intelligence

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-61925-the-dropping
**Date**: June 20, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball highlights the dramatic collapse in inference pricing -- o-series model costs dropped 87% in just a few months -- and argues this trend will continue driving the marginal cost of creation toward zero. He takes a strongly optimistic position on the job displacement debate, arguing that near-zero creation costs will generate massive net new economic activity and jobs, though acknowledges the speed of this transition (vs. the Industrial Revolution) presents real reskilling challenges.

## Key Claims

- OpenAI o-series (o1 to o3) pricing dropped 87% within a few months of early 2025.
- The marginal cost of creation is approaching zero, which Ball calls "profound."
- Multiple large-company CEOs have publicly commented on AI's implications for headcount.
- Ball argues AI will produce net new job creation, not net destruction, driven by near-zero creation costs unlocking new economic opportunities.
- The key risk vs. historical creative destruction (Industrial Revolution) is speed -- reskilling timelines are far shorter.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue at 5.3x (June 2025); median CAC payback at 72 months (elevated).
- Median SaaS operating margin at -4%, indicating the sector still trades growth for profitability.
