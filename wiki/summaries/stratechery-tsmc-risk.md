# TSMC Risk

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/tsmc-risk/
**Date**: January 26, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Thompson reframes the biggest TSMC risk: it is not a Chinese invasion of Taiwan, but TSMC's rational conservatism as a monopoly foundry that offloads capex risk to hyperscalers in the form of foregone revenue. Every major hyperscaler (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) reported demand exceeding supply in their latest earnings; TSMC's flat capex from 2022-2024 despite the ChatGPT-driven AI boom created today's shortage. While TSMC is ramping to $52-56B capex in 2026, this still lags hyperscaler capex growth and won't produce chips until 2028. Thompson argues hyperscalers must stop begging TSMC for more capacity and instead invest in making Intel and Samsung viable competitors -- competitive pressure is the only mechanism that shifts risk back to foundries and unlocks the full AI buildout.

## Key Claims

- TSMC's capex was basically flat from 2022-2024 (declining in both 2023 and 2024) despite the post-ChatGPT AI demand explosion.
- Amazon, Microsoft, Google, and Meta all reported AI demand exceeding supply in their latest earnings -- foregone revenue is real and happening now.
- Azure AI services demand exceeded supply even as Microsoft brought more capacity online (per CFO Amy Hood).
- TSMC's $52-56B 2026 capex, while record-setting, still grows slower than hyperscaler capex, implying the supply gap widens in 2028.
- TSMC capex risk is asymmetric: nearly all costs are fixed (depreciation), so excess capacity means huge losses -- explaining rational conservatism.
- The risk TSMC avoids does not disappear; it transfers to hyperscalers as foregone revenue potentially in the hundreds of billions by end of decade.
- Amodei called selling H200 chips to China "a bit like selling nuclear weapons to North Korea"; Thompson disagrees, arguing China should be kept dependent on TSMC and US chips rather than cut off.
- The geopolitical risk (Taiwan) will ultimately be solved not by insurance against invasion but by hyperscalers realizing TSMC monopoly risk exceeds geopolitical risk.
