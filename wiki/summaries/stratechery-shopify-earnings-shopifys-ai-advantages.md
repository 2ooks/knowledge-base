# Shopify Earnings, Shopify's AI Advantages

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/shopify-earnings-shopifys-ai-advantages/
**Date**: February 18, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Despite missing earnings estimates (shares fell 13%), Thompson argues Shopify is one of the clearest AI winners in software. He identifies six structural advantages: exclusive commerce data (trillions of data points from billions of transactions), AI-driven market expansion (AI search orders up 15x YoY), physical-world interaction that "cubes" the difficulty of displacement, high-risk payment workflows that require trusted intermediaries, a pre-existing transaction-based business model that works regardless of whether humans or agents initiate purchases, and horizontal coverage across the entire merchant stack. Thompson is particularly irritated by analyst panic about agentic shopping, noting that Shopify Payments still processes the backend of every agentic purchase.

## Key Claims

- Shopify revenue grew 31% to $3.7B; GMV reached ~$124B, both beating expectations; shares fell 13% on EPS miss.
- Orders from AI search to Shopify stores are up 15x since January 2025 (from a small base).
- Shop Campaigns revenue doubled in 2025 with merchant adoption tripling; merchants now advertise across 8 channels with zero risk (pay only on conversion).
- Shopify co-developed Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) with Google, covering discovery through fulfillment and returns.
- Shopify supports OpenAI's Agentic Commerce Protocol but is putting all strategic weight behind UCP.
- LLMs doing not bypass Shopify Checkout -- agents handle the front-end UI, but Shopify still runs backend order processing and payments.
- Shopify represents over 14% of U.S. e-commerce.
- AI benefits the long tail of commerce, surfacing niche products that would be invisible through traditional search -- and Shopify is the home of the long tail.
- The Shopify Product Network launched in December, allowing merchants to surface products from other Shopify stores on their own site for a commission.
