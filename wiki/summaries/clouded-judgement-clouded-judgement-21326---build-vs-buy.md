# Clouded Judgement 2.13.26 - Build vs Buy

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-21326-build-vs
**Date**: February 13, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball argues that the real threat to incumbent software is not enterprises "vibe coding" internal replacements -- the classic build-vs-buy objections (TCO, maintenance, compliance) still hold. The actual risk is that near-zero software creation costs flood the market with AI-native competitors purpose-built for agents and consumption pricing, commoditizing legacy categories. He draws a parallel to the newspaper industry circa 2002: markets discounted newspaper stocks for five years before earnings actually cracked, suggesting software incumbents should not take comfort in strong near-term quarterly results.

## Key Claims

- "Vibe coding" eliminates some build-vs-buy friction but does not resolve maintenance, security, compliance, or feature velocity gaps.
- The real disruption risk is 10 new AI-native startups building a CRM from scratch with agent-first UX and consumption pricing, not enterprises building internally.
- Newspaper stocks declined for 5 years (2002-2007) before earnings actually fell -- markets price disruption well ahead of P&L impact.
- Software disruption is more likely to trim growth rates than erase demand entirely, unlike newspapers which lost their core business to zero-cost alternatives.
- Software has stronger network effects, data moats, and switching costs than newspapers did.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue fell to 3.4x (Feb 2026), down from 4.8x in Dec 2025; high-growth median at 9.7x.
- Nokia retained ~$40B market cap even after Apple "killed" them -- disrupted incumbents rarely die completely.
