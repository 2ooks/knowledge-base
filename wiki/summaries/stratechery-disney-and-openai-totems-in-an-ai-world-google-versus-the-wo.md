# Disney and OpenAI, Totems in an AI World, Google Versus the World

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2025/disney-and-openai-totems-in-an-ai-world-google-versus-the-world/
**Date**: December 15, 2025
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Disney invested $1B in OpenAI and licensed 200+ characters for Sora video generation, while simultaneously sending a cease-and-desist to Google for copyright infringement via Gemini. Thompson frames this as emblematic of the Google-vs-everyone dynamic: Google pursues web-like scale without deals, while OpenAI's structural weakness in infrastructure makes deal-making an advantage. The "totems" thesis argues that shared, universally recognized content (Disney characters, Stratechery articles) becomes more valuable in an AI world because AI output is individualized and cannot serve as a common reference point for community formation.

## Key Claims

- Disney invested $1B in OpenAI with warrants to buy more stock at $500B valuation.
- The deal licenses 200+ Disney, Marvel, Star Wars, and Pixar characters for Sora, excluding voice and actor likeness.
- Disney sent a cease-and-desist to Google the day before announcing the OpenAI deal.
- The character license is partially exclusive to Sora for approximately one year.
- Thompson's "totems" thesis: shared content becomes more valuable as AI individualizes information, because it provides common reference points for community.
- Google pursues scale without deals (like web indexing); OpenAI pursues deals as a competitive advantage against Google's infrastructure lead.
- The three AI labs are "stuck in lanes that don't entirely fit": Google has scale but lost the consumer chatbot race; OpenAI has users but needs ads; Anthropic has enterprise but the most consumer-friendly model.
