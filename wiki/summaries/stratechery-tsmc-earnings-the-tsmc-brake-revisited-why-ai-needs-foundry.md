# TSMC Earnings, The TSMC Brake Revisited, Why AI Needs Foundry Competition

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/tsmc-earnings-the-tsmc-brake-revisited-why-ai-needs-foundry-competition/
**Date**: January 21, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

TSMC announced record capex of $52-56B for 2026 (up 27-37% YoY), but CEO C.C. Wei admitted the demand-supply gap remains TSMC's biggest headache. New fabs take 2-3 years to build, so this spending won't meaningfully contribute until 2028-2029, extending the silicon bottleneck that is already constraining hyperscaler revenue. Thompson argues the only way to remove the "TSMC brake" on AI infrastructure buildout is to bring Intel and Samsung online as credible foundry competitors -- not to beg TSMC to invest more, but to shift risk back to foundries through competitive pressure. Wei's confident dismissal of Intel competition (citing 5+ year catch-up timelines) underscores why hyperscalers must invest now in alternative suppliers if they want adequate capacity by end of decade.

## Key Claims

- TSMC plans $52-56B capex for 2026, up from $41B in 2025 -- a record, but with minimal production impact before 2028.
- TSMC CEO Wei confirmed silicon from TSMC, not power, is the bottleneck for AI data center buildout.
- New fabs take 2-3 years to build; 2026 spending primarily targets 2028-2029 production capacity.
- TSMC spent too little on capex in prior years (only $30B in 2024), creating today's supply-demand gap.
- Wei expressed nervousness about whether AI demand is real, after personally vetting hyperscalers' financials and use cases.
- Hyperscalers confirmed to Wei that AI is already driving profitable business growth with healthy financial returns.
- Wei dismissed Intel competition, claiming 5+ years to become a credible alternative -- but Thompson argues 3 years is more realistic.
- The only way to motivate TSMC to invest more aggressively is competitive pressure from Intel/Samsung, not customer requests.
