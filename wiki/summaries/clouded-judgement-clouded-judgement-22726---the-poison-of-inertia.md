# Clouded Judgement 2.27.26 - The Poison of Inertia

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-22726-the-poison
**Date**: February 27, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Triggered by Jack Dorsey's ~40% headcount reduction at Block, Ball argues that organizational inertia is the real killer during platform shifts. Using the Sabre/Travelocity case study, he shows that incumbents historically had to spin up entirely separate entities to escape existing playbook gravity -- because sales comp structures, marketing channels, product cadences, and culture all resisted change. In the AI era, every enterprise playbook (PLG, SDR ratios, hiring profiles) must be rewritten, and modern AI-native vendors will structurally produce software at lower cost, forcing incumbents to match or die.

## Key Claims

- Block announced a ~40% RIF explicitly framed around AI-driven organizational restructuring.
- Sabre created Travelocity as a separate entity because internal inertia made it impossible to build an OTA within the existing org.
- GDS systems went from ~$20/booking to a >10x reduction post-internet/OTA disruption.
- Every enterprise function (sales, marketing, product dev, culture) carries playbook inertia that resists platform transitions.
- In an agentic world, SDR-to-AE ratios shift from 2:1 human to 100 agentic SDRs per 1 human SDR supporting 10 AEs.
- AI-native vendors will produce software at structurally lower cost, forcing incumbents to materially reduce their cost basis.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue at 3.3x (Feb 2026); low-growth median at 2.6x.
