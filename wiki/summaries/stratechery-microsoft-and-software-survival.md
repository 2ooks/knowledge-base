# Microsoft and Software Survival

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/microsoft-and-software-survival/
**Date**: February 3, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

After Microsoft lost $357B in market cap (second-largest single-session destruction ever), Thompson analyzes whether AI will kill the software business model. His answer: no, but AI will trigger a brutal SaaS consolidation war. AI-written code lowers the cost of software creation for everyone equally (like the Internet lowered distribution costs for publishers), meaning growth-via-new-SaaS-verticals is over -- the next decade will be about fighting over adjacencies. Microsoft's strategic position hinges on Work IQ (enterprise identity and organizational data as the agent platform), but the per-seat model is structurally threatened if AI reduces headcount. Microsoft explicitly chose to allocate scarce GPUs to its own products over Azure customers, admitting it could have hit 40% Azure growth if it had allocated all GPUs to Azure.

## Key Claims

- Microsoft lost $357B in market cap in one session, the second-largest single-day value destruction in stock market history.
- Azure growth came in a point below expectations; Hood said Azure could have hit 40% growth if all new GPUs were allocated to Azure.
- OpenAI represents 45% of Azure's Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO).
- M365 Copilot has 15 million paying customers, a tiny fraction of the overall M365 base.
- Thompson argues software companies are winners from AI (they write code faster) but face existential competition from adjacent SaaS players doing the same.
- Active Directory is "one of the most valuable free products of all time" -- Microsoft's identity ownership is the foundation for Work IQ.
- Microsoft's per-seat model is structurally threatened if AI reduces the number of human workers.
- The neocloud/pure-play token foundry value proposition may be larger than previously appreciated, given hyperscalers prioritize their own workloads.
