# Xbox Replaces Head of Gaming, Xbox History, Whither Xbox

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/xbox-replaces-head-of-gaming-xbox-history-whither-xbox/
**Date**: February 25, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Phil Spencer retired after 38 years at Microsoft and was replaced by Asha Sharma, former president of Microsoft's CoreAI Product unit -- a non-gaming executive signaling Microsoft's strategic pivot away from first-party console hardware. Thompson traces Xbox's structural misalignment with Microsoft's strategy from Gates' original "own the living room" thesis through Nadella's software-and-services reorientation, arguing the "Xbox Everywhere" strategy under Spencer/Bond failed (hardware revenue declined three consecutive fiscal years). The appointment of an AI executive to run gaming, combined with Microsoft's need to concentrate all resources on winning in AI, suggests Xbox will transition to a publishing and services business, possibly eventually spinning off, with no future first-party console hardware.

## Key Claims

- Phil Spencer retired after 38 years; replaced by Asha Sharma, previously president of Microsoft CoreAI Product unit.
- Xbox hardware revenue declined for three consecutive fiscal years, and declines are expected to continue through FY2026.
- Sharma explicitly committed to "returning to Xbox" starting with console and hardware, but her only hardware example was the Asus-made Xbox handheld -- a third-party device.
- Sarah Bond, Xbox president, was passed over for the top role despite leading the console strategy under Spencer's direction.
- Thompson predicts Microsoft will never make a first-party console again; the Xbox brand will live on in publishing and third-party OEM hardware.
- Microsoft's AI priorities (Azure, OpenAI partnership, enterprise AI) demand all available capex, engineering, and executive attention -- leaving no runway for console investment.
- Xbox Game Pass may eventually be sunset in favor of selling games at full price to recoup acquisition costs (Activision Blizzard, Zenimax).
- Gates' original Xbox strategy was flawed (living room trojan horse); Ballmer failed to kill it; Nadella inherited a platform with no strategic purpose as merely a console.
