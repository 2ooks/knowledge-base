# Technological Scale and Government Control, Paramount Outbids Netflix for Warner Bros.

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/technological-scale-and-government-control-paramount-outbids-netflix-for-warner-bros/
**Date**: March 3, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Thompson argues that AI's power will inevitably attract government control regardless of who funded its development -- a structural shift from the nuclear era where government funded and thus naturally controlled the technology. Private companies developed AI with private capital, but the government will still demand control or unfettered access. Separately, Paramount Skydance outbid Netflix for Warner Bros. Discovery at $31/share after Netflix declined to match, signaling massive Hollywood consolidation. The combination of Paramount and Warner Bros. is a horizontal merger (both in streaming, cable, studios, theatrical) promising $6B in synergies, which is M&A shorthand for layoffs at scale.

## Key Claims

- AI differs from nuclear weapons: nuclear was government-funded from the start, while AI is developed by private companies on private clouds with private chips -- but government will still demand control.
- Bob Noyce's decision to pursue civilian over military chip markets at Fairchild/Intel created the dynamic where civilian tech far outpaced military tech by the 1980s.
- Paramount Skydance bid $31/share for all of Warner Bros. Discovery; Netflix declined to match.
- Paramount promises $6B in synergies by end of 2029 from what is fundamentally a horizontal acquisition.
- Netflix stock jumped on news it lost the deal -- market relief that Netflix avoided overpaying and regulatory risk.
- Thompson argues the Paramount-WBD merger should clear antitrust review because consolidation is the natural response in a secularly declining industry, and the real competitive space is attention broadly.
