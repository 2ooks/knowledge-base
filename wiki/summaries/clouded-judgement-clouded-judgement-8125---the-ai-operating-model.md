# Clouded Judgement 8.1.25 - The AI Operating Model

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-8125-the-ai-operating
**Date**: August 1, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball argues that the real AI competitive advantage is not better models or chatbots but a fundamentally new operating model -- companies that embed AI into every department (engineering, GTM, support, forecasting) compound speed advantages that incumbents structurally cannot match. He draws a direct parallel to the SaaS transition, where startups that adopted inside sales and self-serve funnels outran field-sales incumbents, and cites Anthropic's own Claude Code usage for growth marketing as a concrete example. The piece also covers Q2 2025 cloud earnings: Azure at ~$86B run rate growing 39% YoY, AWS at $123B growing 17%, and Google Cloud at $54B growing 32%.

## Key Claims

- The lasting AI advantage is operational model change, not product features -- companies that restructure around AI will outpace those that bolt it on.
- Startups win platform shifts not by being more agile at coding, but by adopting entirely new playbooks before incumbents can shed legacy org structures.
- Azure grew 39% YoY to an estimated ~$86B run rate in Q2 2025, accelerating from 35% the prior quarter.
- AWS hit $123B run rate at 17% YoY growth; Google Cloud reached $54B run rate at 32% growth.
- SaaS median NTM growth rate is 11%; median FCF margin is 18%; high-growth SaaS companies (>25% growth) trade at 23.3x NTM revenue.
- Keith Rabois argues that hiring "industry veterans" optimized for old playbooks may be counterproductive in AI-native companies.
