# AI News & Notes: Week of Sep 8 -- $300B Bets, Memory Wars, and FTC Heat

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/ai-news-and-notes-week-of-sep-8-300b
**Date**: September 13, 2025
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

A weekly roundup covering six major AI stories, headlined by Oracle's $300B five-year cloud deal with OpenAI (starting 2027) and Anthropic's launch of Team Memory for Claude enterprise accounts. The Oracle deal signals OpenAI's "soft divorce" from Microsoft toward multi-cloud, while Claude's project-isolated memory with transparent tool calling positions Anthropic to compete directly with project management tools. Also covers the AI agent market projected to surge from $5B to $40-50B by 2030, and OpenAI's hallucination research that acknowledges training-level causes but is unlikely to change current engagement-optimized training regimes.

## Key Claims

- Oracle signed a $300B, five-year cloud computing deal with OpenAI beginning in 2027 -- one of the largest contracts in tech history.
- Oracle stock popped 40% on the announcement, making Larry Ellison temporarily the world's richest person.
- The deal represents OpenAI's strategic move from Microsoft-first to multi-cloud dependency.
- Anthropic launched Team Memory for Claude (Sep 9-11) with project-isolated memory and transparent tool calling (conversation_search, recent_chats).
- AI agent market projected to surge from ~$5B (2025) to $40-50B by 2030.
- Agent-to-agent collaboration (e.g., Genesys Agent2Agent) will be a key focus area in 2026.
- OpenAI's hallucination research attributes hallucinations to pre-training prioritizing word prediction over truthfulness -- known but now formally acknowledged.
- FTC launched AI chatbot inquiry targeting seven companies (OpenAI, Meta, Google, Snap, Character.AI, xAI) focused on child safety.
