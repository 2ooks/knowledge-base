# Why High Agency Is a Non-Negotiable in 2026 (Especially If You're Entry Level)

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/the-career-ladder-collapsed-heres
**Date**: January 22, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter

## Summary

Argues that AI has structurally dismantled the traditional career ladder by automating the low-risk repetitive tasks that once trained entry-level workers, creating a gap where organizations need experienced people but have eliminated the mechanism that produces experience. Frames "high agency" (internal locus of control + high say-do ratio) as the only viable career strategy in 2026, with AI acting as the most powerful accelerant for agency ever created. Uses solo founder data and TSMC CapEx trends to ground the argument in market structure.

## Key Claims

- Entry-level hiring at major tech companies collapsed by more than 50% since 2019 (SignalFire data).
- Recent college graduate unemployment at 5.3% (Q3 2025) vs. 4.4% overall unemployment (BLS, December 2025).
- Solo founders rose from 23.7% of startups in 2019 to 36.3% in H1 2025 (Carta data).
- Maor Shlomo built Base44 from side project to ~$80M Wix acquisition in six months with no venture funding and ~8 people.
- Dario Amodei and Sam Altman both predict the first one-person billion-dollar company could emerge as early as 2026.
- AI unicorns reached billion-dollar valuations in ~2 years with ~200 employees vs. 9 years and nearly double the team for non-AI unicorns.
- Meta-analyses (Ng et al. 2006, Wang et al. 2010) consistently link internal locus of control with greater persistence, better professional outcomes, and stronger entrepreneurial activity.
