# State of the Product Job Market in Early 2026
- **Source:** https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/state-of-the-product-job-market-in-ee9
- **Date:** March 2026 [UNVERIFIED]
- **Author:** Lenny Rachitsky

## Summary
Lenny Rachitsky's data-driven assessment of the tech job market in early 2026 presents a notably optimistic picture that contradicts prevailing media narratives about AI-driven job displacement and widespread layoffs. The analysis shows product management roles at their highest level in over three years (7,300+ global openings, up 75% from early 2023 and 20% year-to-date), with AI-specific roles experiencing explosive 340% growth since 2024. Engineering positions remain strong with 67,000 global openings and a ~3:1 job-to-candidate ratio. However, the recovery is characterized as "selective" — Growth PM roles are the fastest-growing PM category, certain engineering specializations (Python, cloud infrastructure, API design, CI/CD) command premium demand, while generalist mid-market PM roles face saturation. Design roles have plateaued as the only major tech discipline not seeing growth, and remote work opportunities continue declining in favor of hybrid/in-person arrangements, reinforcing the importance of tech hub proximity.

## Key Claims
- PM job openings reached 7,300+ globally in early 2026 — highest in 3+ years, representing a 75% increase from early 2023 and 20% growth year-to-date [UNVERIFIED]
- Growth PM roles (scaling/user acquisition focus) are now the fastest-growing PM category, surpassing AI-adjacent PM titles in new opening velocity [UNVERIFIED]
- AI-specific roles grew 340% since 2024, described as "hockey-sticking" with ongoing acceleration [UNVERIFIED]
- Software engineering openings reached 67,000 globally (26,000 U.S.), up 11% year-over-year, with approximately 3 engineering jobs per qualified candidate [UNVERIFIED]
- In-demand engineering specializations: Python, cloud infrastructure, API design, CI/CD pipelines; saturated areas: generalist JavaScript roles [UNVERIFIED]
- AI is expanding total tech employment rather than cannibalizing existing engineering roles — "expanding the pie" framing [UNVERIFIED]
- Job market recovery is "selective and competitive" — senior IC and leadership PM roles can go unfilled for months despite high opening counts, while generalist mid-market PM roles face applicant saturation [UNVERIFIED]
- Design roles are the only major tech discipline showing plateau/stagnation rather than growth [UNVERIFIED]
- Remote work opportunities continue declining, signaling shift back toward hybrid/in-person and renewed importance of tech hub geography (Bay Area emphasis) [UNVERIFIED]
- The market shows "robust if uneven recovery" — headline job counts up significantly, but individual job seeker experience remains slow and highly competitive [UNVERIFIED]
