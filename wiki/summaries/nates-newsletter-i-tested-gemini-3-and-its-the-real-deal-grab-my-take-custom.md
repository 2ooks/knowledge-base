# I Tested Gemini 3 And It's The Real Deal: Grab My Take + Custom Prompt to See The Impact on Your Role

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/how-gemini-3-changes-your-job-a-practical
**Date**: November 19, 2025
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter (Substack)

## Summary

Gemini 3's multimodal capabilities -- particularly visual understanding and agentic screen interaction -- force a shift from "which model is best?" to "which model handles which work?" The article argues organizations need a dedicated routing layer and AI platform group to direct visual/agentic tasks to Gemini 3 while keeping writing-heavy work on Claude Sonnet 4.5 or GPT 5.1. This is strategically significant because it signals the end of single-model loyalty and the emergence of model orchestration as a core enterprise function, directly relevant to how Microsoft positions Copilot alongside third-party frontier models.

## Key Claims

- Gemini 3 is dominant at visual and agentic tasks (UI debugging, video analysis, design QA) but unremarkable at persuasive writing and everyday chat.
- The strategic question shifts from "which frontier model" to "how you route between them" -- someone must own the routing layer as an actual function.
- Organizations still identifying as "an OpenAI shop" or "an Anthropic shop" are solving the wrong problem.
- ROI calculation: replacing 3 hours of manual designer screen audits with 10 minutes of Gemini + 20 minutes of human review makes token cost negligible.
- Gemini 3's million-token context window shifts effort from curating context to designing better queries and output formats.
- The hard skill is now specification and review, not prompt engineering -- evaluating agent-produced artifacts rather than writing code directly.
- Teams should loop in security, data, and IT early on agentic workflows as design partners, not last-minute gatekeepers.
