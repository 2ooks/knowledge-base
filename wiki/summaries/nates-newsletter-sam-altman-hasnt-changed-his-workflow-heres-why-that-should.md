# Sam Altman Hasn't Changed His Workflow. Here's Why That Should Terrify You.

**Source URL**: https://natesnewsletter.substack.com/p/the-capability-overhang-is-real-5
**Date**: February 4, 2026
**Publication**: Nate's Newsletter (Substack)

## Summary

Despite OpenAI's internal benchmark (GDP-val) showing AI output preferred over human expert output 74.1% of the time on scoped tasks, even Sam Altman admits he hasn't changed his workflow. The article documents the December 2025 convergence -- three frontier model releases in six days, the Ralph and Gas Town orchestration patterns going viral, and Claude Code's task system making both obsolete within weeks. The resulting "capability overhang" between what AI can do and what people actually use it for creates a temporary but closing arbitrage window. This is directly relevant to how Microsoft/GitHub positions developer tooling: the value is not in models but in orchestration layers that close the adoption gap.

## Key Claims

- OpenAI's GDP-val benchmark: GPT-5.2 Pro output preferred over human experts 74.1% of the time on well-scoped knowledge tasks.
- Andrej Karpathy's workflow inverted from 80% manual coding to 80% AI agents in weeks during December 2025.
- Three frontier releases in late November: GPT-5.1-Codex-Max (24-hour autonomous operation), Claude Opus 4.5 (~67% cheaper), Gemini 3.
- Cursor used GPT-5.2 to build a web browser from scratch: 3M+ lines of code generated in ~1 week.
- OpenAI announced plans to "dramatically slow down" hiring pace, reasoning AI will amplify smaller teams.
- Anthropic engineers report "I don't write any code anymore" -- the self-acceleration loop where AI builds better AI.
- Claude Code's task system killed the Ralph pattern by providing native platform infrastructure for hierarchical multi-agent work.
- The specification skill is becoming more valuable than the coding skill: "describing a system precisely enough that an AI can build it."
