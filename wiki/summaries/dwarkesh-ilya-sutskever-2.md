# Ilya Sutskever — We're Moving from the Age of Scaling to the Age of Research
- **Source:** https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/ilya-sutskever-2
- **Date:** ~November/December 2025
- **Host:** Dwarkesh Patel
- **Guest:** Ilya Sutskever (Co-founder, Safe Superintelligence Inc.)

## Summary
In this second Dwarkesh Podcast interview, Ilya Sutskever declares that "the age of scaling" — characterized by making bigger models with more data — is ending, because "the data is finite; there is only one internet; pre-training as we have known it is over" [UNVERIFIED]. He predicts a shift to an "age of research" driven by synthetic data, self-generated training problems, and fundamentally new algorithms [UNVERIFIED]. On alignment, Sutskever admits it is hard and unresolved, requiring "radical new scientific understanding," and has shifted from a "build in secret then unveil" approach to a gradualist deployment philosophy where society provides crucial oversight [UNVERIFIED]. SSI's strategy is to pursue fundamental research with massive funding but no product or commercial focus until safety confidence is achieved [UNVERIFIED]. A central theme is the generalization gap: current models "generalize dramatically worse than people," excelling at benchmarks through rote study rather than genuine flexible learning, and Sutskever frames solving this as SSI's core research problem [UNVERIFIED].

## Key Claims
- "The data is finite. There is only one internet. Pre-training as we have known it is over." [UNVERIFIED]
- Three eras of AI: 2012–2020 research-driven, 2020–2025 scaling-driven, 2026+ research-driven again [UNVERIFIED]
- Next breakthroughs require fundamentally new algorithms — "straight shot" scaling of transformers is hitting diminishing returns [UNVERIFIED]
- Future approaches: synthetic data, models learning through self-generated problems, continual learning, more generalizable architectures [UNVERIFIED]
- SSI has massive funding but no product — research-first, deployment only after safety confidence [UNVERIFIED]
- Alignment requires "radical new scientific understanding" — Sutskever is skeptical of claims that robust alignment solutions currently exist [UNVERIFIED]
- Shifted to gradualist approach: incremental deployment so society can adapt and provide oversight [UNVERIFIED]
- Current LLMs "generalize dramatically worse than people" — the gap between benchmark performance and real-world capabilities is a core research problem [UNVERIFIED]
- Timeline for superintelligence: 5–20 years [UNVERIFIED]
- Gap between AI benchmark scores and real-world economic impact is explained by the generalization problem [UNVERIFIED]
