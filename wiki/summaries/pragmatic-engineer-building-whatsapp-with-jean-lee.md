# Building WhatsApp with Jean Lee

**Source URL**: https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/building-whatsapp-with-jean-lee
**Date**: March 18, 2026
**Publication**: The Pragmatic Engineer

## Summary

Engineer #19 at WhatsApp recounts how 30 engineers served 450 million users with zero formal process -- no Scrum, no Agile, no TDD, no ongoing code reviews -- while outcompeting Skype's 1,000-engineer team. The episode is a case study in how small, experienced, high-trust teams with ruthless feature discipline can outperform process-heavy organizations at orders-of-magnitude scale. For BD, it challenges the assumption that AI tools are the primary lever for engineering efficiency; WhatsApp's example suggests ownership models and team composition may matter more than tooling.

## Key Claims

- WhatsApp served 450 million users with only 30 engineers, pre-AI tooling era
- Only 4 of 30 engineers were under 30 years old at the time of the $19B Facebook acquisition in 2014
- WhatsApp had no code reviews after the first PR, which was reviewed in extreme detail by cofounder Brian Acton
- CEO Jan Koum rejected 99% of internal feature requests
- WhatsApp charged $1/year, which was sufficient to pay all salaries while keeping growth intentionally slow
- WhatsApp used Erlang for the backend, avoided cross-platform abstractions, and supported 8 platforms natively
- Jean believes AI will handle OKR management and documentation but not the human elements of engineering management
