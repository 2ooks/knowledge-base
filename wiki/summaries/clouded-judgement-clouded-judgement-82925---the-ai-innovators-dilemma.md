# Clouded Judgement 8.29.25 - The AI Innovators Dilemma

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-82925-the-ai-innovators
**Date**: August 29, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball reframes the classic innovator's dilemma for AI labs: the binding constraint is no longer just people and attention but physical compute. Labs like OpenAI and Anthropic must ration GPU capacity across serving, training, and research, forcing them to protect proven products (ChatGPT, coding) at the expense of emerging categories. This creates structural openings for startups that are narrowly focused, compute-efficient, and free from internal allocation battles. The argument is that compute scarcity functions as a new organizational constraint that tips advantage toward fast-moving, single-domain entrants.

## Key Claims

- Compute rationing is a daily scheduling decision inside large AI companies, forcing tradeoffs between serving vs. training vs. research and between protecting the cash cow vs. chasing new vectors.
- Incumbents with flagship products (ChatGPT, coding agents) absorb disproportionate compute share just to maintain latency and reliability, starving experimental products.
- Startups win by choosing a narrow job, shaping models/prompts/data around it, and achieving lower compute-per-user waste.
- Startups can arbitrage GPU supply across clouds and regions with lower SLO commitments than large brands.
- Coding agents are cited as an example of how fast a category can tip -- being late by even a few months means missing the wave.
- Signals of compute constraint biting: persistent waitlists, quiet throttling, price/quality tier shifts, and more partnerships/licensing in hot categories.
- SaaS median valuation: 5.1x NTM revenue; high-growth median: 30.5x; median CAC payback: 34 months.
