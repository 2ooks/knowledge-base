# Clouded Judgement 1.9.26 - The Education Advantage in AI

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-1926-the-education
**Date**: January 9, 2026
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball argues that education -- not product features -- is the primary competitive advantage in enterprise AI right now. Most organizations face a "blank canvas problem" where they know AI is powerful but cannot decide where to start or how to build. The winning AI vendors are those that teach the market how to think about the problem, not just what their product does. This creates a distinctive GTM pattern where adoption starts slow (education cannot be rushed) but accelerates dramatically once mental models click and teams shift from experimentation to standardization.

## Key Claims

- The biggest enterprise AI GTM challenge is education, not product differentiation -- prospects face a "blank canvas problem."
- Build vs. buy is just the starting point; the deeper education gap is about architectural philosophy (opinionated vs. composable, managed vs. self-hosted).
- No vendor can talk a customer out of building internally; teams must experience failure themselves before choosing to buy.
- AI companies compete on worldview, not just features -- each product encodes a point of view about how AI systems should be built.
- Free tiers and sandboxes function as educational tools that accelerate the shift from abstract reasoning to concrete understanding.
- Once users internalize the right mental model, buying decisions "snap into place" and revenue ramps can be extraordinary.
- SaaS median EV/NTM revenue: 4.7x; high-growth median: 13.9x (Jan 2026).
