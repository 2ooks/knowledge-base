# Clouded Judgement 6.27.25 - New Rules For a New Game

**Source URL**: https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/clouded-judgement-62725-new-rules
**Date**: June 27, 2025
**Publication**: Clouded Judgement (Jamin Ball / Altimeter)

## Summary

Ball argues that the traditional SaaS startup playbook -- raise sequentially, de-risk in stages, hire proven operators -- is dangerously slow in AI's greenfield markets. With categories still forming and first-movers like Cursor hitting escape velocity before competitors can react, speed of execution now outweighs polish. This means doing things in parallel (GTM before PMF, multiple product experiments, strategic hires before strategy is locked), avoiding rigid roadmaps, and hiring rising leaders who can write new playbooks rather than importing "old guard" VPs who ran the previous era's motions.

## Key Claims

- In AI greenfield markets, the first company to get real usage becomes the category and sets the norms.
- Cursor developed mindshare and hit escape velocity in coding so fast that competitors who "blinked" are already far behind.
- The old sequential approach (milestones then raise, de-risk then expand) is too slow; parallel execution is now required.
- Long-term product roadmaps may be counterproductive -- the world changes so fast that roadmaps create bias toward outdated plans.
- Hiring the "VP of Sales from [successful public company]" is now a risk; new playbooks require leaders unconstrained by old-era thinking.
- "Perfection is the enemy of good" -- ship with core message right, then iterate; speed of execution outweighs polish.
- SaaS high-growth median EV/NTM at 21.8x (June 2025); median CAC payback at 72 months.
