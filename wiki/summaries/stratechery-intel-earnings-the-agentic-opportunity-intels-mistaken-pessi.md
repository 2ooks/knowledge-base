# Intel Earnings, The Agentic Opportunity, Intel's Mistaken Pessimism

**Source URL**: https://stratechery.com/2026/intel-earnings-the-agentic-opportunity-intels-mistaken-pessimism/
**Date**: January 27, 2026
**Publication**: Stratechery

## Summary

Intel shares plunged 17% after reporting manufacturing problems and weak guidance, but the underlying demand story is bullish: agentic AI workloads are driving soaring demand for server CPUs because agent orchestration, tool invocation, and data lookups are CPU-bound tasks. Intel's problem is self-inflicted -- new CEO Lip-Bu Tan tore down older manufacturing capacity (Emerald Rapids/Granite Rapids lines) just as hyperscalers like OpenAI, AWS, and Google realized they needed more and better CPUs for AI data centers. Intel's cautious approach to 14A capacity (waiting for secured customers before spending) may be the wrong strategy when the risk of missing upside exceeds the risk of overinvestment.

## Key Claims

- Intel shares fell 17% on weak Q1 guidance and manufacturing yield problems.
- Agentic workflows dramatically increase CPU demand: tool invocations, data lookups, and orchestration are all CPU-bound.
- COVID-era data center CPUs (now ~5 years old) are too slow for agentic workloads, driving greenfield replacement demand on both agent and data delivery sides.
- Intel retired Emerald Rapids (Intel 7/10nm) and Granite Rapids (Intel 3/7nm) manufacturing tools, then couldn't meet surging demand from OpenAI, AWS, and Google.
- Intel's monolithic chip designs (vs. AMD's chiplet approach) make capacity reallocation slower.
- Nvidia's Grace Blackwell has one CPU per two GPUs; previous Grace Hopper was 1:1 -- GPUs always need CPUs.
- Intel CFO said the company won't spend on 14A fab capacity until customers are secured in H2 2026/H1 2027.
- Thompson argues Intel's problem shifted from Gelsinger's excessive optimism to Tan's excessive pessimism.
