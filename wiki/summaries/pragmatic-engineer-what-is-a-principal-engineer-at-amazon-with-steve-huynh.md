# What is a Principal Engineer at Amazon? With Steve Huynh

**Source URL**: https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/what-is-a-principal-engineer-at-amazon
**Date**: July 9, 2025
**Publication**: The Pragmatic Engineer

## Summary

Former Amazon Principal Engineer Steve Huynh details 17 years at the company, including the extreme difficulty of the Senior-to-Principal promotion, Amazon's scale challenges (10K-100K+ requests per second), and the engineering practices that emerge from operating at that scale -- including latency-as-revenue research, "brownout" management for cascading failures, and the 6-pager writing culture. The BD relevance is in understanding Amazon's engineering DNA: the leadership principles, writing culture, and "freedom of movement" internal transfer policy shape how Amazon builds and evaluates technology partnerships, and why their engineering org values operational excellence above all.

## Key Claims

- Senior to Principal Engineer is one of the hardest promotions in tech at Amazon
- Amazon handles 10K-100K+ requests per second across its services
- "Brownout" is Amazon internal terminology for partial service degradation (vs. full "blackout" outage)
- Amazon's internal research directly tied latency to revenue impact, making latency a company-wide obsession
- Amazon's "freedom of movement" policy allows engineers to transfer across teams (Kindle, Prime Video, payments)
- The Principal Engineering community is described as one of the most rewarding parts of the role
- Amazon's COE (Correction of Error) process institutionalizes learning from mistakes
- Amazon's 6-pager writing culture requires detailed written proposals over slide presentations
- Amazon patents software as a deliberate business strategy
- Steve recommends starting with a monolith before adopting microservices
