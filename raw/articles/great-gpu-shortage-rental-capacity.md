# The Great GPU Shortage – Rental Capacity
**Source:** https://newsletter.semianalysis.com/p/the-great-gpu-shortage-rental-capacity
**Date:** April 1, 2026
**Authors:** Daniel Nishball, Jordan Nanos, Cheang Kang Wen, et al. (SemiAnalysis)

## Summary
Anthropic's Claude 4.6 Opus and Claude Code have soared in demand. Anthropic's ARR has nearly tripled in a single quarter from $9B to over $25B. Open models like GLM and Kimi K2.5 caused open model use cases to soar. Capital raises by Anthropic, OpenAI, and various Neolabs also demand GPUs.

H100 1-year GPU rental contract pricing has shot up almost 40% from a low of $1.70/hr/GPU in October 2025 to $2.35/hr/GPU by March 2026. On-demand GPU rental capacity is sold out across all GPU types.

## Key Data Points
- H100 1Y rental: $1.70 → $2.35/hr/GPU (+40%) Oct 2025 to Mar 2026
- All on-demand capacity sold out across all GPU types
- Blackwell lead times extending into June-July 2026
- All capacity coming online until Aug-Sep 2026 already booked
- SemiAnalysis consumed billions of tokens (~$5/M tok avg) in past 7 days
- Claude Code projected to be 20%+ of all daily commits by end of 2026

## Market Drivers
- Multi-agent workloads executing multi-step workflows at high concurrency
- Native media generation (Seedance, Nano Banana) driving massive token throughput
- Memory pricing going parabolic: LPDDR5 and DDR5 contract prices tracking ~4-5x YoY
- OEMs repricing AI servers above underlying component cost increases
- Return on AI tools is 5-10x the cost → long way to go before prices curtail demand

## GPU Rental Market Structure
1. **Short-term** (On-Demand, Spot, <3 months): Residual capacity, fixed pricing adjusted infrequently based on utilization
2. **Mid-term** (3 months to 3+ years): Most sensitive indicator of tightening; AI Natives and smaller labs
3. **Long-term** (4-5 year): AI labs locking 50-100MW+ clusters; 5-year most popular; often backstopped by hyperscalers

## SemiAnalysis H100 1Y Price Index
- Constructed from survey of 100+ market participants monthly
- Validated by transaction data and facilitated transactions
- Made publicly available at semianalysis.com/gpu-pricing-index

## Outlook
- GPU rental pricing more likely to continue rising than falling
- Self-reinforcing dynamic: tighter supply → higher prices → more hardware pre-buying → tighter supply
- Neoclouds with shorter contract tenors benefit from faster repricing
- CoreWeave, Nebius, IREN share prices at low end of trading range despite fundamentals improving
