# The Fourth Industrial Revolution: The Intelligence Revolution
**Source:** https://cloudedjudgement.substack.com/p/the-fourth-industrial-revolution
**Date:** July 17, 2024
**Author:** Jamin Ball (Clouded Judgement / Altimeter)

## Summary
Innovation follows exponential curves. The gap between industrial revolutions has been compressing: 110 years (1st→2nd), 90 years (2nd→3rd), and ~55 years (3rd→4th). The Fourth Industrial Revolution — the Intelligence Revolution — is defined by the transition from human-driven processes to AI-driven intelligence. We are only ~5 years in, with the majority still ahead.

Data center buildouts are the foundational infrastructure of this revolution. While GPUs get the attention, there are many other critical components. This post explores the 11 essential components of data centers.

## Key Framing
- 1st Industrial Revolution (~1760): Mechanized production
- 2nd Industrial Revolution (~1870): Electricity, internal combustion, railways, telegraph
- 3rd Industrial Revolution (~1960): Computers, digital technology, internet
- 4th Industrial Revolution (~2017-2022): AI-driven intelligence
- AlexNet (2012), Attention Is All You Need (2017), ChatGPT (2022) as markers

## 11 Essential Data Center Components

### 1. Servers
- CPUs (Intel Xeon, AMD EPYC), GPUs (Nvidia, AMD), Memory/RAM (Samsung, Micron, SK Hynix), HBM, Storage (HDDs, SSDs, NVMe)

### 2. Networking
- Routers (Cisco, Juniper), Switches (Arista, Broadcom), Firewalls (Palo Alto, Fortinet), Load Balancers (F5, Citrix), Interconnects/Fiber Optics (Corning, CommScope, Coherent, Amphenol)

### 3. Storage Systems
- SAN (Dell/EMC, NetApp), NAS (Synology, QNAP), DAS (Dell, HPE)

### 4. Cooling Systems
- CRAC Units (Schneider Electric, Vertiv), In-row Cooling (Stulz, Rittal), Liquid Cooling (Asetek, CoolIT), Chillers (Trane, Johnson Controls)

### 5. Power Supply
- UPS (Eaton, APC/Schneider), PDUs (Raritan, Server Technology), Backup Generators (Generac, Caterpillar)

### 6. Security
- Physical (Honeywell, Bosch), Cyber (Palo Alto, Symantec)

### 7. Management and Monitoring
- DCIM (Schneider Electric, IBM), Remote Monitoring (Paessler, SolarWinds)

### 8. Racks and Cabinets
- Server Racks (APC, Panduit), Network Racks (Chatsworth, Eaton)

### 9. Cabling
- Copper (Belden, CommScope), Fiber Optic (Corning, OFS, Amphenol)

### 10. Environmental Controls
- Humidity Control (Honeywell, Johnson Controls), Fire Suppression (Tyco, Siemens)

### 11. Software
- Virtualization (VMware, Microsoft), Management (IBM, HPE), Backup/Recovery (Veeam, Commvault)

## Key Bottlenecks Not Covered
- Real estate and power proximity (power loss during transmission favors co-location)
- Natural gas could play a big role in on-site generation
- Nuclear, solar storage, alternative energy all needed for breakthroughs
