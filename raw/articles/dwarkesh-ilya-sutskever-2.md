## Core thesis

Ilya Sutskever declares that "the age of scaling is over." Pre-training on static internet data has reached diminishing returns — "there is only one internet." Progress now requires the "age of research": new algorithmic breakthroughs, synthetic data generation, and models that learn from deployment and real-world interaction rather than only from pre-training corpora.

## Jagged generalization

Current models exhibit "jagged generalization" — they can ace graduate-level benchmarks while failing at simple reasoning or basic debugging loops. This unevenness reflects over-optimization via RL on verifiable benchmarks, risking overfitting to those benchmarks rather than developing genuine general capabilities.

## RL limitations

RL is consuming increasing compute relative to pre-training but yields only modest learning gains. This raises fundamental questions about whether RL-at-scale is a genuine shortcut toward AGI or a more limited technique for capability extraction from already-trained models.

## Safe Superintelligence Inc.

SSI is structured research-first. Alignment and safety are not afterthoughts — they are core design constraints from the start. The stated goal is AI that inherently values "caring for sentient life," rather than having safety bolted on post-training.

## The unknown principle

A "specific, currently unknown machine learning principle" is needed to achieve efficient, robust generalization akin to human cognition. Sutskever frames this as the central research problem of the current era — not a compute problem.

## Scaling headroom

Another 100× compute scaling might move the needle on current model capabilities but would not fundamentally transform what these systems can do. Algorithmic innovation is essential, not optional.

## Deployment philosophy

Superintelligence deployment should be gradual, with systems continually learning from real-world use rather than being deployed fully-formed. This aligns with SSI's research-first approach and implies a different deployment cadence from "release-and-scale" labs.
