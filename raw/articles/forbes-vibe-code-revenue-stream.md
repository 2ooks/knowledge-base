## Vibe coding a new revenue stream from existing expertise

### Building at a rate that is "just insane"

"I live in Claude Code every day building software at a rate that is just insane," says Evan G., software builder and founder. "I can do in a day what used to take a month."

Evan describes having his own content team, design team, and development team, all through Claude Code, and is simultaneously building patty.com, brooke.com, and racingminds.com. He no longer writes code at all.

The speed changes the economics of testing ideas. A product you're not sure will sell used to require months of commitment before you found out. Use Wispr Flow to dictate your prompts, describe the product in detail, and have a testable version in front of your existing audience by the end of the week. The cost of being wrong dropped to almost nothing.

### From one idea to multiple live products

"100% Claudepilled over here. My entire life and business is run from the command line," says Arthur Kerekes, entrepreneur and AI builder. In the span of a few months, Kerekes launched an AI photo booth platform with 20,000 styles and subscriptions at bananacam.ai, a cross-platform content pipeline with nine specialised agents producing on-brand content at contentsidekick.ai. But also, a LinkedIn outreach system sending personalised connection requests autonomously, and a Gmail marketing system that created hundreds of personalised email drafts with tracking and attachments in a single session. Every single one shipped to production.

Before you start building, get clear on who you're building for and what specific outcome they want. Load your ICP document and brand guidelines at the start of every session. That clarity is what turns a build into something with a sales page behind it. Your existing audience already tells you what they'd pay for if you listen to what they keep asking.

### A 30-day content marathon that became a product engine

Dhanyal Radha Gopi is founder of a video accelerator programme. He recently completed a 30-day video marathon, publishing one video a day across YouTube, Instagram, and LinkedIn. Claude helped him compile the performance data into a public insights page. He then fed the stats back into Claude to create platform-specific videos optimised for two distinct goals, using what the data showed actually worked.

Most founders treat content as a one-directional output. Radha Gopi treated it as a research cycle. The data from thirty days of publishing became the brief for the next build. Upload your content performance data into Claude Cowork and ask it what your audience is telling you. The answer shapes the product, the format, and the platform before you've written a single prompt about what to build.

### An applied framework turned into a live product

"I am extremely claudepilled and am working on a gstack framework that applies to product management," says Chad Thomas, a software builder for accounting firms. The framework is live, built and shipped rather than left as a document or a LinkedIn post. Most founders with established frameworks don't take this step.

You have methods you've refined over years. You explain them in calls, reference them in proposals, teach them in workshops. Those frameworks sitting as documents or slides are products waiting to be packaged. Take the one you explain most often and build the version someone can use without you in the room. Ship it to the ten people most likely to find it useful. Their response tells you everything about whether to scale it.

## Build new revenue from what you've already built

They looked at what they already had: their knowledge, their audience, their frameworks, and asked what it would take to package it properly. That question, answered honestly, is where most new revenue streams begin. Yours is probably closer than you think. Vibe coding just handed non-technical founders technical superpowers. Don't waste this opportunity.
